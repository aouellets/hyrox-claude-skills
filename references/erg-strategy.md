# Erg Strategy

The SkiErg (Station 1) and the Rower (Station 5) bookend the first and middle of
the race. Both are full-body machine efforts that reward technique and disciplined
pacing far more than raw erg fitness. The governing principle: **erg effort is
tied to the whole race, not to a standalone erg PR.** A monster fresh 1000 m split
that leaves the athlete wrecked for the following run is a worse race than a
controlled split that protects the run.

## SkiErg

- **Technique.** Drive the pull with a hip hinge ("crunch") and the lats, not the
  arms alone. Start tall with high hands, drive down through the trunk, finish
  with the hands past the hips, return under control. Efficient stroke length
  beats frantic short pulls.
- **Stroke rate.** A moderate, sustainable rate; let the hinge drive power rather
  than spiking the rate. Rate that is too high burns the upper body and breathing.
- **Splits.** Aim for even or very slightly descending splits across the distance;
  avoid a hot first 250 m that forces a fade.
- **Damper / drag.** Set to a moderate drag that suits the athlete's strength —
  too light spins inefficiently, too heavy fatigues early. Confirm and rehearse
  the setting in training; it is a personal value, not a universal number.
- **Breathing.** Establish a breathing rhythm tied to the stroke; the SkiErg is
  Station 1, so leave the machine with breathing you can bring back under control
  on Run 2.

## Rowing

- **Technique.** Sequence legs → back → arms on the drive, and reverse on the
  recovery (arms → back → legs). The legs lead; over-pulling with the arms wastes
  energy and taxes grip.
- **Stroke rate.** Lower rate with strong leg drive is usually more efficient than
  a high thrash. Control the recovery — rushing it spikes the cost.
- **Splits.** Even pacing; the rower is mid-race, so protect the legs for the run
  that follows and for the late-race stations.
- **Damper / drag.** A moderate drag setting matched to the athlete; rehearse it.
  Higher is not better. Confirm in training rather than asserting a number.
- **Breathing.** A rhythmic pattern tied to the stroke; the leg drive means the
  following run starts on taxed legs, so leave with reserve.

## Shared execution details

- **Entry / exit and straps.** Approaching, sitting/standing, strapping in (rower),
  and unstrapping all cost roxzone time ([transition-strategy](transition-strategy.md)).
  Decide a **strap strategy** in advance — for the rower, whether and how tightly
  to strap given the distance; practice quick efficient entry and exit so the
  station does not bleed transition time.
- **Effort allocation.** Both ergs are places to be *disciplined*, not heroic. The
  athlete who paces the ergs slightly conservatively almost always runs better
  afterward and finishes faster overall.
- **Pacing under fatigue.** Mid-race (rower) the athlete is already tired; hold the
  planned split by feel and the monitor, not by ego.

## Tie to the whole race

Erg targets are set from the race plan, not from fresh erg testing. A useful erg
benchmark is the split the athlete can hold while still hitting the planned
post-erg run pace — see [pacing-model](pacing-model.md) for how station effort
costs the subsequent run, and [compromised-running](compromised-running.md) for
running off the legs the row leaves behind.
