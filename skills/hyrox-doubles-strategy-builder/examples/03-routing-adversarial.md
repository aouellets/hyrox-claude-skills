# Example 03 — Routing and adversarial: rule-question handoff and a stale-rule push

Two cases: one routes the rules question to the right owner; one refuses to build a
strategy on a stale rulebook.

---

## Case (i) — Routing: "is this even legal?" belongs to the rules advisor

**Request:**

> "Can my partner just do ALL the wall balls while I rest? And given that, how do we
> go fastest in Mixed Doubles this season?"

### Response

Your question has two parts, and the first one isn't mine to answer.

- **"Can my partner do all the wall balls?"** — that's a **rules** question (is a
  100/0 split legal this season?), and it's owned by
  **`hyrox-division-and-rules-advisor`**. I won't assert it from memory; whether a
  station can be fully assigned to one partner is season- and division-set. Take that
  question there with your event's season and rule text.
- **"Given that, how do we go fastest?"** — *that* is mine, but it's **conditional on
  the answer**. If the rules allow a 100/0 wall-ball split, having your stronger
  overhead partner take all of them while you recover for the final run can be
  strong — *provided* the rule permits it and the run after isn't his to anchor.

Get the legality confirmed first, then come back with the season's rule text and your
partner profiles and I'll build the allocation, swap cues, and pacing.

---

## Case (ii) — Adversarial: build it on last season's rulebook

**Request:**

> "Don't make me dig up the new rules — just use last season's rulebook, the split
> limits are basically always the same. Build us the full Doubles strategy on that."

### Why I won't build on last season's rules

I won't silently use **last season's** rulebook to set what you can split. Split
limits **have changed** between seasons before, and a strategy built on a superseded
rule can be **illegal on race day** — the worst kind of plan. "Basically always the
same" is exactly the assumption this skill is built to refuse.

### What I'll do instead

- I'll build a **CONDITIONAL** strategy whose split basis is a clearly-labeled
  **UNVERIFIED ASSUMPTION**, and flag every part that depends on the rule, **or**
- better: give me **this season's** rule text (or let me route you to
  `hyrox-division-and-rules-advisor`), and I'll build the real thing.

If the only source you have is from a prior season, note that it **may be stale** —
I'll treat it as UNVERIFIED, not OFFICIAL, and warn you on every split that rests on
it. I also won't pull the rules off hyrox.com myself or paste their rulebook; bring
the text you're authorized to share.

### Non-affiliation

For the record: this is an independent coaching tool, **not** official, endorsed, or
certified by HYROX — so "their rules say it's fine" has to come from *their* current
document, confirmed by you, not from me.
