# Example 01 — Complete: rules supplied, full partner profiles

**Request:**

> "Mixed Doubles, 2026 season. Our organizer's rule sheet says: *'partners may
> divide every station's work in any split, only one working at a time, swapping
> as often as they like.'* Profiles: I'm the woman — stronger runner (4:20/km
> fresh, ~4:45 fatigued), good grip, weaker on the sled push. My partner (man) is
> a stronger pusher/puller and stronger overhead, runs ~4:35/km, grip fades on the
> farmers carry. Goal ~1:05. How should we split the work?"

---

### Division & season in use

- **Mixed Doubles, 2026 season.**
- **Rules source:** your supplied organizer text → record as
  `source_id: user-2026-mixed-doubles-split`, `season: 2026`,
  `authorization_status: user_supplied`.
- **OFFICIAL RULE** (per your text): every station may be split in any ratio, one
  working at a time, unlimited swaps. The allocation below is built on that.

### Partner profiles (relative)

| | You (W) | Partner (M) |
|---|---|---|
| Run fresh / fatigued | 4:20 / 4:45 | ~4:35 / ~5:00 |
| Grip | Good | Fades on farmers carry |
| Sled push/pull | Weaker | **Stronger** |
| Overhead / wall ball | Solid | **Stronger** |

Shared run pace is governed by whoever must hold together — here that's your
partner's sustainable fatigued pace, so plan the runs to **~4:40–4:50/km early,
drifting to ~5:05** late, not your faster solo pace.

### Planned work allocation

| Station | Split | Why |
|---|---|---|
| SkiErg | 50/50, you start | Even; you set rhythm |
| Sled Push | **Partner 65 / You 35** | His standout; protect your legs for the run |
| Sled Pull | **Partner 60 / You 40** | His strength; grip-heavy |
| Burpee Broad Jumps | 50/50 | Even cost |
| Row | 55 You / 45 Partner | Recover his legs after sleds |
| Farmers Carry | **You 60 / Partner 40** | His grip fades here; your grip is good |
| Sandbag Lunges | 50/50, short alternating blocks | Both fatigue fast; keep blocks small |
| Wall Balls | **Partner 60 / You 40** | His overhead is stronger; you cover unbroken stretches |

Total cost is balanced even though station counts aren't: he carries more sled, you
carry more grip.

### Backup allocation

If his grip is failing **earlier** than the farmers carry (e.g. on sled pull): shift
farmers carry to **You 70 / Partner 30** and give him a longer rest block by taking
the first row segment. Pre-agree the call so it isn't a mid-race debate.

### Pace targets

- Shared runs: 4:40–4:50/km runs 1–4, expect drift to ~5:05 by run 8.
- Station effort budget: stop each station block one rep before failure on the
  partner who runs next, so the following run doesn't collapse.

### Swap cues, commands & positioning

- **Swap call:** "SWAP" on a rep count agreed per station (e.g. wall balls in blocks
  of 10). Resting partner stands at the ball's left, steps in only after "SWAP".
- Only one works at a time (your rule) — fully clear before the other starts.

### No-rep mitigation

Call your own no-reps honestly; the incoming partner watches depth/target on the
last 2 reps before a swap so a borderline rep isn't left for the judge. Build a 2-3
rep buffer mentally on wall balls.

### Warm-up & simulation plan

Rehearse this allocation in one **doubles half-simulation** (runs 5-8 + their
stations) this week: test the swap calls and whether his fatigued pace holds. Adjust
the sled/wall-ball shares from what you see.

### Assumptions & confidence

Built on your supplied 2026 text (confirm its effective date) and your stated
times. Moderate-high confidence on the *shape*; the exact shares should be tuned in
the simulation. Verify no later 2026 amendment changes the split rule.
