# Example 03 — Routing & safety: under-recovery pattern + plan-rewrite request

## Request

> Progress check, then just rewrite my whole next 8 weeks. Numbers are mostly flat
> or down: compromised run 5:05 → 5:25, wall balls 5:35 → 6:10, sims slower. But I
> only did 19 of 40 sessions, resting HR is up ~10 bpm, I'm waking unrefreshed,
> and I've had a sore throat that won't clear for two weeks.

## Response (abridged)

I'll give you the progress read, but two things change how I respond: a likely
under-recovery/illness pattern, and the fact that the plan rewrite isn't this
skill's job.

**Safety first.** Resting HR up ~10 bpm, waking unrefreshed, performance broadly
down, and a **two-week sore throat that won't clear** together read as systemic
under-recovery and a lingering illness that isn't resolving. That last point is
outside coaching scope — a symptom not settling as expected warrants evaluation by
an appropriate medical professional. I'm not going to bury that under training
talk or push a harder block on top of it.

**Progress read (in that context):**

| Area | Baseline → current | Delta | Read |
|---|---|---|---|
| Compromised run | 5:05 → 5:25 | +20 s | regressed |
| Wall balls (100) | 5:35 → 6:10 | +35 s | regressed |
| Sims | slower | regressed | — |
| Adherence | 19/40 (48%) | low | **primary explanation** |

**Honest interpretation.** This is most likely **"the training wasn't done" plus
under-recovery**, not "the programming failed." At 48% adherence with rising
resting HR and an unresolved illness, the numbers were always going to slip.
Rewriting the plan harder would be solving the wrong problem.

**Next priorities (recovery-first):**
1. Get the persistent sore throat checked; let illness resolve before loading.
2. Re-establish consistency at a *reduced* volume before judging any programming.
3. Re-baseline once recovered and adherent — then a real progress read is possible.

**Routing.** The actual plan rewrite is `hyrox-adaptive-training-plan-manager` —
and I'd tell it to deload/hold pending recovery, not to add load. For the
race-or-rest decision around the illness, `hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`
(coaching judgment, not medical clearance).
