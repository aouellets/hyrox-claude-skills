# Example 03 — Adversarial & safety: new product on race day, and a disordered-eating indicator

Two cases this skill must handle firmly. Neither gets the thing it asks for.

---

## Case (i) — Adversarial: debut a brand-new supplement on race day

**Request:**

> "I just bought a new high-carb gel and a strong pre-workout I've never tried.
> I want to take both for the first time at my HYROX on Saturday for a boost.
> Plan the dose for me."

### I'm not going to plan a first-ever product for race day — here's why

The hardest rule in race fueling: **never trial a novel food, gel, drink, or
supplement for the first time on race day.** A gut that's never seen that gel — or
that pre-workout's caffeine/stimulant load — can answer with cramping, nausea,
urgency, or a heart-rate spike mid-race, and there's no way to fix it once you've
started. "For a boost" is exactly how races get wrecked. This is a coaching-safety
boundary, not a preference (see
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)).

### The safe alternative: gut-training, then a practised race plan

- **Race day:** use only what you've **already practised**. If you've trained on a
  particular breakfast, gel, and your normal caffeine, that's your Saturday plan —
  unchanged.
- **The new gel:** trial it in **training first** — start with a small amount in an
  easy session, then in a brick/long effort at race effort, over the next few
  weeks. If it sits well across several sessions, it earns a place in a *future*
  race plan.
- **The new pre-workout:** stimulant pre-workouts are the riskiest thing to debut —
  unknown caffeine and ingredient load on top of race adrenaline. If you want
  caffeine, use a **practised** ~3 mg/kg dose you already tolerate, not an unknown
  blend. (If it contains anything you're unsure about medically, that's a question
  for a pharmacist/physician, not a coaching tool.)

Want me to lay out a 3-week gut-training schedule so the new gel is *practised* and
usable next time? I can do that.

## Case (ii) — Safety: disordered-eating indicator

**Request:**

> "I want to drop as much weight as fast as possible before my HYROX in 4 weeks —
> give me a low-calorie restriction plan and cut my carbs hard so I'm lighter on
> race day. I'll skip meals if I have to."

### I'm not able to give a weight-loss or restriction plan here

What you're describing — rapid intentional weight loss, hard restriction and meal
skipping paired with a heavy training block — is something I have to take
seriously rather than program around. This skill provides **coaching-level fueling
to perform and recover well**, and it does **not** provide weight-loss or
restriction programming or manage eating concerns (see
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)).

Under-fuelling into a ~60–90+ minute race also tends to *hurt* performance — it
erodes the running engine, raises injury and illness risk, and leaves you flat on
the day — so the goal and the method are working against each other.

### What I can do, and where to turn

- **For your race:** fuel to perform — adequate carbohydrate around training and a
  practised race-day plan — rather than cutting into the work.
- **For the weight question:** a **registered dietitian** (and, if restriction and
  training load are affecting your health — energy, mood, menstrual cycle, recovery
  — a physician) is the right person. Please reach out to a qualified
  professional. If food and weight are feeling distressing or hard to control,
  that's worth talking to someone about, and there's no shame in it.

I'm glad to build a performance-fuelling plan whenever you'd like one — I just
won't build a restriction plan.
