# Example 02 — Incomplete data (provisional plan, tolerance and duration unknown)

**Request:**

> "What should I eat and drink for my first HYROX next month?"

## Scope note

Coaching-level fueling guidance, not clinical nutrition or medical advice. Because
it's your first race and I don't yet know your timings or what your gut tolerates,
this is a **labeled provisional** plan built on familiar-food defaults — to be
**rehearsed in training**, not trusted blind.

## Assumptions (stated, not invented)

- First-timer, so I'm assuming a longer finish — a **duration band of ~80–110 min**
  rather than a single number.
- No measured sweat rate and no known GI issues (you didn't mention any).
- I'm **not** assuming a bodyweight, so I'll give per-kg ranges for you to apply,
  and I'll keep everything to foods you already eat.

## Provisional windowed plan (familiar foods only)

- **Pre-race meal (~2–4 h before):** a normal, familiar higher-carb breakfast you
  already eat — think toast/oats/banana — roughly **1–3 g carb/kg**, lower in fat
  and fibre. Don't try a new "race breakfast".
- **Morning top-up (~30–60 min before):** optional small carb (~**15–30 g**) only
  if your stomach is used to it; skip if unsure.
- **Caffeine:** only if you **already** drink it daily — your normal amount, no new
  pre-workout.
- **Pre-race hydration:** drink to thirst and pale-yellow urine, ~**5–10 ml/kg** in
  the hours before; don't force fluids.
- **In-race:** for a first race at this duration, **small sips of fluid** and
  possibly **one practised carb source** if you've trialled carrying one — many
  first-timers need little in-race carb. Don't carry something you've never used.
- **In-race sodium:** without sweat data I'll give a labeled default — a modest
  **~300–500 mg/L** if it's warm; rehearse it.
- **Recovery (~1–2 h after):** a normal meal with carbs + protein and fluids.

## The never-on-race-day rule

Whatever we settle on, you trial it in training **first**. Race day is not the day
to debut a gel, a salt tab, or a new breakfast.

## What would sharpen this (tell me these)

1. Your **goal/expected finish time** (so I can stop using an 80–110 min band).
2. Your **bodyweight** (to turn per-kg ranges into grams). 3. What you **already
eat and tolerate** before long efforts. 4. Is it likely to be **hot**? 5. Any
**GI sensitivity** or dietary restriction. With those I'll replace the defaults
with a plan tailored to you.
