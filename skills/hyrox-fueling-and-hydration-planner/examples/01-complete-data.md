# Example 01 — Complete data (full fueling plan, well-practised athlete)

**Request:**

> "HYROX Singles in 3 weeks, goal ~1:25, I'm 72 kg. Race is likely warm (~26 °C).
> I regularly do long efforts on a banana + oats breakfast and take one gel mid-way
> in training; I use coffee daily and tolerate it fine. I've measured my sweat
> rate at roughly 1.2 L/h in the heat and I'm a salty sweater. Build my race-day
> fueling and hydration."

## Scope note

This is **coaching-level** fueling guidance, not clinical nutrition or medical
advice. Everything below is built from products you've **already practised** — and
nothing new goes in on race day.

## Assumptions

- ~1:25 effort (~85 min), 72 kg, warm (~26 °C), measured sweat ~1.2 L/h, salty
  sweater, caffeine-habituated, oats/banana and one gel already tolerated.
  **Confidence: high** — you've given duration, tolerance, and sweat data.

## Windowed plan (ranges; all already practised)

| Window | Plan | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-race meal (~3 h before) | Your oats + banana, ~**1.5–2 g carb/kg** ≈ **110–145 g carb**, low fat/fibre | familiar, tolerated |
| Morning top-up (~45 min before) | Optional ~**15–25 g** carb (e.g. half a banana or a few sips of your sports drink) if your gut likes it | practised |
| Caffeine (~45–60 min before) | ~**3 mg/kg ≈ 200 mg** — within your daily habit; not a new dose | habituated |
| Pre-race hydration (2–3 h) | ~**5–7 ml/kg ≈ 360–500 ml** to pale-yellow urine; don't over-drink | thirst + urine colour |
| In-race carb | ~**1 gel (~20–25 g)** around the middle, as you practise; ~85 min + heat justifies it | tolerated in training |
| In-race fluid/sodium | Small sips where practical; in the heat aim for the higher sodium end, ~**500–700 mg/L**, because you're a salty sweater at ~1.2 L/h | sweat data |
| Recovery (within 1–2 h) | ~**1 g carb/kg ≈ 72 g** + ~**25–35 g protein** + rehydrate with sodium | standard recovery |

## The never-on-race-day rule

Every item above is something you already use. Do **not** add a new gel, a
stronger pre-workout, a "race-special" drink, or extra caffeine on the day — a
sudden change is the classic way to gift yourself GI distress mid-race.

## What to rehearse and when

Run your two key long/brick sessions in the next 3 weeks on exactly this plan,
including the warm-weather sodium, so race day is a copy of a session you've
already done.

## What would sharpen it

Nothing critical — your sweat data already does the heavy lifting. If the forecast
shifts hotter, nudge fluid and sodium up (still within practised products).
