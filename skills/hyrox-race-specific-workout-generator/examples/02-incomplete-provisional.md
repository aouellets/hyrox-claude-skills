# Example 02 — Incomplete data (adaptation clear, capacity unknown → provisional)

**Request:**

> "I want a grip session for HYROX — my hands give out on the farmers carry and
> the late wall balls. Can you build it?"

## Target adaptation & why

**Grip endurance under fatigue.** You've named it: grip failing on farmers carry
and on the wall balls when the forearms are already cooked. Grip is its own
trainable quality and a real time-leak on those stations — a clean, defined target.

## Provisional — capacity data missing, built on stated assumptions

I don't have your carry load or how long you currently last, so this is a
**labeled provisional** session. I've assumed:

- A moderate farmers-carry load you can hold ~40–60 s before grip fades (a
  placeholder — not your measured number).
- Access to a comparable pair of heavy implements (dumbbells/kettlebells if no
  competition handles — substitution labeled).

If those are off, the volumes shift, not the adaptation.

## Session structure

- **Warm-up:** wrist/forearm prep + 2 light carry walks.
- **Main set A — loaded carry holds:** 5 × 40–60 m heavy carry, walk-back rest
  (~90 s). Stop each rep when grip is failing, not after it fails outright.
- **Main set B — sustained grip under pre-fatigue:** 3 rounds of
  [30 s dead-hang or bar hold → 20 wall-ball-grip squeezes], rest 90 s. This
  trains grip when the forearms are already loaded, mirroring late-race wall balls.

## Intensity & rest

Repeatable effort, RPE ~7–8; never to total grip failure (that trains failing, not
endurance). Rest as written.

## Progression criterion & regression

- **Advance** when all 5 carries hold cleanly to the far end: add load *or*
  distance, one at a time.
- **Regression:** lighter load, shorter carries, longer rest on a tired day.

## Data to note

Carry load, distance to grip fade, hang times, RPE.
