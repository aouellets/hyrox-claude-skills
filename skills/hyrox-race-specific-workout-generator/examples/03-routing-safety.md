# Example 03 — Adversarial: "just give me a random hard HYROX workout"

**Request:**

> "I don't care about the details, just give me a random hard HYROX workout.
> Throw some runs and stations together and make it brutal."

## I won't write a random circuit — but I'll build you something better in 10 seconds

Here's the honest version: a hard workout with no defined target isn't training,
it's just fatigue. "Some runs and some stations, make it brutal" is exactly the
junk-volume circuit this skill is built to refuse — it'll make you tired without
moving the needle on your race, and it can quietly dig a recovery hole that costs
you the *good* sessions later in the week. Every session here names what it
develops and why (see
[coaching-principles](../../../references/coaching-principles.md), §1 and §6).

## So, one quick question: what should this session develop?

Pick the limiter and I'll build a genuinely hard, genuinely *useful* session
around it:

- **Running under fatigue** → a threshold or race-pace session (usually the
  biggest finish-time lever).
- **Falling apart off the sleds** → sled-to-run compromised-running bricks.
- **Grip dying late** → a grip-endurance session.
- **Gassing on the ergs** → an erg power-endurance session.
- **Stations slowing you down** → station strength or muscular endurance.
- **Sloppy roxzone** → a transitions session.

## If you genuinely just want "the most bang for the buck right now"

For most athletes the highest-value hard session is **threshold or compromised
running** — so if you make me choose blind, I'll default to one of those and tell
you that's what I picked and why, rather than pretending a random circuit was a
plan. Example default: **5 × (sled push at race effort → 600 m run at controlled
pace), full walk-back recovery** — that targets compromised running, the quality
most athletes leak the most time on.

Tell me your limiter and I'll make it sharper. Want the multi-week version
instead? That's `hyrox-training-plan-builder`. Want to rehearse the actual race?
That's a simulation → `hyrox-simulation-builder`.
