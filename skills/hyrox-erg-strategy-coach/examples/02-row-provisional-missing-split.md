# Example 2 — Incomplete data: Rower help, no current split or run context

**User:**
> My row feels awful mid-race and my legs are dead after it. How should I pace it and
> what split should I hold?

**Response:**

This is a **provisional** rowing plan. I can coach the mechanics fully right now, but
I can't hand you a *split number* yet — and here's exactly why.

## What I'm missing (and why it blocks the split, not the coaching)
1. **A current erg reference** — a recent 1000 m row time, or a 500 m split you can
   sustain. Without it I'd be inventing a number, which I won't do; I'll open with a
   baseline test instead.
2. **Your run paces** — fresh 1 km, and ideally what your run *feels* like off the
   row. The Rower is **Station 5, mid-race**: the right split is the one that still
   lets you run after it, so the split is set *from the following run*, not from a
   fresh erg PR.

Tell me those two and I'll give you a target split tied to your Run 6.

## Rowing coaching (fully actionable now)
Your "dead legs after" is the tell: you're likely **over-pulling with the arms** and/or
**rushing the recovery**, both of which spike the cost.

- **Sequence:** legs → back → arms on the drive; arms → back → legs on the recovery.
  **Legs lead.** Over-pulling the arms wastes energy and taxes grip you need later.
- **Rate:** lower rate, strong leg drive beats a high thrash. **Control the recovery** —
  rushing it back is what spikes the cost and cooks the legs.
- **Drag/damper:** moderate, matched to you, *rehearsed* — higher is not better. I'm
  not asserting a number; confirm yours in training.
- **Breathing:** rhythmic, tied to the stroke; leave reserve because the drive has
  already loaded the legs you're about to run on.

## Strap & entry/exit
Decide your strap approach in advance for the row distance and rehearse a quick
strap-in/strap-out so you don't bleed roxzone time.

## Provisional rehearsal (baseline first)
- **Week 1 baseline:** 1000 m row for time + note the run feel after a controlled
  400 m. That gives me your starting split and your compromised run pace.
- Then: 2×/week **row-to-run bricks** — row at the planned race effort → controlled
  400–600 m run, holding the run pace. Splits filled in once you send the baseline.

## Retest
Repeat the baseline in ~4 weeks: target a **faster run off the same row effort**.
