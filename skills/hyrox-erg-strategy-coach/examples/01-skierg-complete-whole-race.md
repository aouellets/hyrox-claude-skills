# Example 1 — Complete data: SkiErg pacing tied to the whole race

**User:**
> Coach my SkiErg. Fresh I can hold a 1:55/500m split for 1000 m (so ~3:50), but
> when I open the race on it my Run 2 is a disaster — I crawl the first 400 m. Fresh
> 1 km run is 4:20, goal finish is around 75 min. Race in 7 weeks. Drag's at 6.

**Response:**

Here's the key move: your problem isn't the SkiErg, it's that you're skiing it like a
**standalone PR** when it's **Station 1 of a race**. A 3:50 that costs you 40s on Run 2
is slower overall than a 4:00 that lets you run. We set the split *from the run*, not
from the monitor.

## Erg context & whole-race framing
- SkiErg, opening station. Fresh capacity ~1:55/500m (3:50 for 1000 m).
- Fresh run 4:20/km; the cost lands on **Run 2**, which is the run you're losing.
- **Target: a race-protecting split, not your fresh PR.** Drag 6 is an *assumption*
  carried from your message — keep it only if it's rehearsed and repeatable for you.

## Limiter read
Not technique-blind necessarily, but the dominant fault is **pacing**: a hot open on
Station 1 floods you for Run 2. Stroke-rate spike and an arms-dominant pull usually
ride along with it.

## Technique (SkiErg)
- Drive the pull with a **hip-hinge "crunch"** through lats and trunk — start tall,
  high hands, finish past the hips, control the return. Long efficient strokes beat
  frantic short pulls.
- **Stroke rate:** moderate and sustainable; let the hinge make the power. If you're
  spiking the rate to hold the split, the rate is the leak.

## Split, rate & shape
- Target a **~4:00–4:05** opening split (≈ 2:00–2:02/500m), **even or very slightly
  descending** — no hot first 250 m. That's ~10–15s "slower" than fresh and it buys
  back far more than that on Run 2.
- Leave the machine with breathing you can bring under control in the first 200 m of
  Run 2 — not gasping.

## Breathing & effort
Rhythm tied to the stroke; exhale-focused. This is a **discipline** station, not a
hero station. You should step off thinking "I had a bit more" — that's correct.

## Entry/exit
SkiErg has no straps, so the cost is the approach and grab — rehearse a quick clean
entry; don't sprint in and arrive already redlined.

## Erg-to-run rehearsal (prove it)
2×/week for the first 3 weeks:
- **SkiErg 1000 m at 4:00–4:05 → immediately 600 m run at 4:25–4:30/km** (your
  *compromised* Run-2 pace, deliberately a touch under fresh for the first 200 m to
  let cadence and breathing settle).
- **Progression criterion:** you hold the run pace and recover turnover inside 200 m.
  When that's easy, nudge the erg split toward 3:57 — but only if the run holds.

## Retest
Week 4: repeat the brick. Win condition is **a faster Run-2 split off the same erg
effort**, not a faster erg time. Re-test the full thing in ~6 weeks before race.

If anything in your chest or breathing feels wrong (not just hard) on the ergs, stop
and get it checked — that's outside coaching.
