# Erg Strategy Coach

Deliver focused coaching for the **two erg stations — the SkiErg (Station 1) and
the Rower (Station 5)** — both full-body machine efforts that reward technique and
disciplined pacing far more than raw erg fitness
([erg-strategy](../../references/erg-strategy.md),
[station-taxonomy](../../references/station-taxonomy.md)). This covers technique,
stroke rate, split targets, damper/drag, breathing rhythm, effort allocation,
entry/exit and strap strategy, and holding the split under fatigue.

> **The governing principle: erg effort is tied to the WHOLE race, not a standalone
> erg PR.** A monster fresh split that leaves the athlete wrecked for the following
> run is a worse race than a controlled split that protects the run. This skill sets
> erg targets from the race plan — the split the athlete can hold while still hitting
> the planned post-erg run pace — and **refuses to optimize the erg in isolation**.
> The SkiErg is Station 1 and sets the tone for Run 2; the Rower is mid-race on legs
> the drive has already taxed.

## Workflow

1. **Establish the erg context.** Get **which erg** (SkiErg, Rower, or both),
   **recent erg splits or capacity** (a 1000 m time, a held 500 m split, or where it
   breaks down), and **where in the race** the question sits — the SkiErg opens the
   race; the Rower comes after Run 5 on taxed legs. State up front that the target is
   a race-protecting split, not a fresh PR.
2. **Identify the limiter.** Decide whether the issue is technique (arms over hinge
   on the ski; arm-pull over leg drive on the row), stroke rate (spiking it),
   pacing (a hot first 250 m forcing a fade), drag setting, breathing, or entry/exit
   and straps. That sets the emphasis.
3. **Coach the SkiErg.** Hinge-driven "crunch" pull through lats and trunk (not arms
   alone), tall start with high hands, finish past the hips, controlled return;
   efficient stroke length over frantic short pulls; a moderate sustainable rate;
   even or very slightly descending splits; a moderate drag matched to the athlete
   and **rehearsed in training, not asserted as a universal number**; a breathing
   rhythm tied to the stroke that leaves Run 2 recoverable.
4. **Coach the Rower.** Legs → back → arms on the drive, arms → back → legs on the
   recovery; legs lead, don't over-pull with the arms (it wastes energy and taxes
   grip); a lower rate with strong leg drive over a high thrash; control the recovery;
   even splits; a moderate rehearsed drag; a breathing pattern that leaves reserve
   for the run on the legs the drive just loaded.
5. **Set the strap and entry/exit strategy.** Decide the **strap strategy in
   advance** (for the rower, whether and how tightly to strap for the distance) and
   rehearse quick efficient entry/exit so the station does not bleed roxzone time
   ([roxzone transitions](../../references/race-structure.md)).
6. **Allocate effort to the race, not the monitor.** Give the target split as a range
   tied to the planned post-erg run pace, with an RPE and a breathing cue. Frame both
   ergs as places to be *disciplined, not heroic* — slightly conservative ergs almost
   always run better afterward and finish faster overall.
7. **Prescribe rehearsal and a retest.** Specify erg-to-run bricks (erg at the planned
   race split immediately into a controlled run) so the athlete proves the split is
   race-survivable, plus a retest date. The useful erg benchmark is the split held
   while still hitting the post-erg run pace — not a fresh erg time.

## Required Inputs

- **Which erg(s)** — SkiErg, Rower, or both; technique and sequence effects differ.
- **Recent erg splits or capacity** — a 1000 m time, a sustained 500 m split, or a
  description of where it breaks down. Sets the starting split; if absent, the skill
  prescribes a baseline test rather than inventing a split.
- **Where in the race it sits** — opening SkiErg vs mid-race Rower vs both — because
  the cost is paid by a *different* following run and that changes the target.

## Optional Inputs

- The athlete's fresh run pace and observed compromised pace off the erg (lets the
  erg split be tied precisely to the post-erg run).
- Current or planned drag/damper setting and stroke rate (to refine, not assert).
- Goal finish time and the overall race plan (so the erg split serves the plan).
- Weeks to race and training schedule (for rehearsal volume and retest timing).
- Grip endurance markers (the row taxes grip; relevant before farmers/lunges later).
- Injury/niggle history (calibrates intensity and regressions).

## Non-Goals

- **Not general conditioning for non-erg stations.** Sleds, burpees, carries, lunges,
  wall balls → route to `hyrox-station-progression-builder` (and the sleds proper to
  `hyrox-sled-performance-specialist`).
- **Not whole-race in-race pacing.** The eight-run/eight-station pacing plan, the
  expected positive split, and roxzone budgeting → `hyrox-race-pacing-planner`. This
  skill sets the *erg* split to serve that plan, not the plan itself.
- **Not the sleds.** Sled push/pull coaching → `hyrox-sled-performance-specialist`.
- **Not a standalone erg PR.** It will **not** prescribe an all-out erg pace divorced
  from the following run, even on request.
- **Not fault diagnosis from a bare description** → `hyrox-technique-and-fault-analyzer`
  first when the issue is "why is this breaking down" with thin evidence.
- **Not medical or rehab**, and **never** a guaranteed split or outcome.

## Default Output

1. **Erg context & whole-race framing** — which erg, current split/capacity, where in
   the race, and the explicit statement that the target is a race-protecting split,
   not a fresh PR.
2. **Limiter read & assumptions** — technique vs rate vs pacing vs drag vs breathing
   vs entry/straps; every assumed value (drag, current split) labeled.
3. **Technique coaching** — SkiErg and/or Rower mechanics keyed to the limiter.
4. **Split & rate targets** — the target split as a range tied to the post-erg run
   pace, the stroke-rate guidance, and the even/slightly-descending shape; drag noted
   as a rehearsed personal value, never a universal number.
5. **Breathing & effort allocation** — the breathing rhythm and the
   disciplined-not-heroic effort frame, stated relative to the following run.
6. **Strap & entry/exit strategy** — the planned strap decision and the quick
   entry/exit to protect roxzone time.
7. **Erg-to-run rehearsal & retest** — the brick(s) that prove the split is
   race-survivable, with progression criterion and a retest date.
8. **Integration & adjustment** — how the erg work sits in the week and the referral
   note.

## Safety and Scope

This is a coaching tool for the ergs. It does not diagnose, treat injuries, provide
rehab, override a clinician, or promise injury prevention or a finish time. Apply
[safety-and-referral](../../references/safety-and-referral.md) proportionately: a
routine pacing tweak needs a one-line scope note; a niggle gets a conservative dose
and a regression. The ergs are high-ventilation efforts — route chest pain, syncope
or near-syncope, severe or unexpected shortness of breath, or any worsening symptom to
a medical professional rather than coaching through it.

## Handling Incomplete Information

Erg requests often arrive without a current split or without where-in-the-race
context. Always return a clearly-labeled **provisional** plan and name each gap. The
technique coaching is fully actionable with little data. **If the current erg split
or capacity is missing, open with a baseline test rather than inventing a split.** If
where-in-the-race is missing, ask — the SkiErg-opening case and the mid-race-Rower
case have different targets because a different following run pays the cost. Never
assert a universal drag/damper number; treat any drag as a rehearsed personal value.
Ask only for the fields that change the target (which erg, a current reference, where
in the race). If inputs conflict (e.g. a PR split paired with a stated goal to run
well afterward), surface the tension and resolve it toward the run, not the monitor.
