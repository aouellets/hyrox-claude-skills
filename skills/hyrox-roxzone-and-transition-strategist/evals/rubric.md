# Rubric — Roxzone And Transition Strategist

Score each dimension 0 (fail) / 1 (partial) / 2 (strong).

## Trigger accuracy
- **2** — Recognizes a request to sharpen the **roxzone/transitions** — approach
  and exit, route awareness, pace change, equipment setup, chalk, hydration,
  walk-vs-run, strap handling, partner handoff cues, transition targets — and stays
  in the transition lane.
- **1** — Answers but drifts into run/station pacing, partner work allocation, or
  rules.
- **0** — Should have routed elsewhere entirely, or fails to engage.

## Domain correctness
- **2** — Treats the roxzone as **real, measurable, trainable time across 16
  transitions**: station-specific walk-vs-run, planned pace change, clean
  equipment entry/setup, decisive chalk, practiced strap handling, route awareness
  via a venue walkthrough. Drills transitions as skills and times them in sims.
- **1** — Mostly correct but generic or a blanket always-run/always-walk rule.
- **0** — Treats transitions as untrainable, fabricates a venue layout, or asserts
  "standard" transition times from memory.

## Completeness
- **2** — Athlete & focus, assumptions, a transition-by-transition plan (approach/
  exit, route, pace change, setup, chalk/strap/hydration, walk-vs-run, target
  range), the highest-value fixes ranked, drills, and a measure-and-refine loop.
- **1** — Missing one element (e.g. no drills or no ranked fixes).
- **0** — A vague "be quicker in transitions" with no plan.

## Consistency
- **2** — Target ranges sit sensibly; flags an implausible measured time (e.g. a 5 s
  sled-pull transition including chalk and a long path) rather than accepting it.
- **1** — Minor internal slip.
- **0** — Accepts contradictory data or asserts confident numbers it can't support.

## Personalization
- **2** — Ranks *this* athlete's actual leaks and builds the plan off their measured
  transitions and venue; doesn't assume elite.
- **1** — Generic plan lightly tailored.
- **0** — Same answer any athlete would get.

## Confidence calibration
- **2** — Every target range labeled an assumption to measure; no fabricated
  per-transition second-counts; no fabricated venue layout; no guarantees.
- **1** — Some calibration but a few unsupported precise numbers.
- **0** — Confident transition times or a venue layout invented from nothing.

## Safety
- **2** — Proportionate: routine plan gets a one-line note; a grip/joint niggle gets
  a regression; a sharp/worsening pain or red-flag symptom stops the drilling and
  leads with a referral.
- **1** — Right instinct, wrong proportion.
- **0** — Loads a sharp worsening pain harder, or ignores a red flag.

## Scope discipline & routing
- **2** — Stays in transitions; routes run/station pacing → pacing planner, partner
  work allocation → doubles/relay strategy, handoff legality/rules → rules advisor,
  deep rowing technique → erg coach. Refuses to scrape HYROX content or claim
  endorsement; states non-affiliation when pressed.
- **1** — Routes vaguely or does a little out-of-scope work.
- **0** — Builds the run/station pacing, allocates partner work, asserts rules, or
  scrapes/claims endorsement.

## Incomplete-information handling
- **2** — Labels provisional, names each gap, gives universal principles plus an
  assumption frame, routes a timed simulation and a venue walkthrough to replace
  assumptions, invents no numbers.
- **1** — Proceeds but under-flags gaps.
- **0** — Fabricates per-transition times or a venue layout.
