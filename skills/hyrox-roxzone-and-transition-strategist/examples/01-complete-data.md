# Example 01 — Complete data: measured sim transitions and a venue walkthrough

## Request

> Singles, I did a full simulation last week and timed the transitions. They're
> slow. Run-out after the sleds I dawdle, the row strapping costs me, and I make a
> leisurely chalk stop before the sled pull. Measured roxzone times (in seconds):
> SkiErg in 10 / out 9; Sled Push in 8 / out 22; Sled Pull in 18 / out 12;
> Burpees in 9 / out 14; Row in 16 / out 11; Farmers in 10 / out 9; Lunges in 9 /
> out 19; Wall Balls in 11 / out 8. I race at this venue and can walk it Friday.
> Tighten my roxzone.

## Response (abridged)

**Athlete & focus.** Singles, with **measured** transition times — so these are
your numbers, not assumptions. **Confidence: high** for the diagnosis; the target
ranges below are still ranges to re-measure after drilling. Total measured roxzone
time ≈ **4:25** across 16 transitions. Realistic, drilled target ≈ **3:15–3:30** —
roughly **a minute** with no extra fitness.

**Your three biggest leaks (fix these first):**

| Transition | Measured | Target range | Fix |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sled Push → out | 22 s | 12–15 s | You're recovering too long. Power-walk 5–8 s to reset legs, then resume — see the post-sled handoff in the pacing plan |
| Lunge → out | 19 s | 12–15 s | Same: brief power-walk, restore tall posture, resume |
| Sled Pull → in (chalk) | 18 s | 8–11 s | Pre-chalk decisively, not a leisurely stop; chalk on the move where you can |
| Row → out (straps) | 11 s + the in-fumble | smoother | Decide your strap-off sequence now and drill it |

**Transition-by-transition plan (targets are ranges to re-measure):**
- **SkiErg in/out (10/9):** already clean. Jog in, smooth onto the handle, no change.
- **Sled Push out (22→12–15):** biggest single fix. The deadness is real and
  *planned* in pacing — but standing in it is wasted time. Power-walk briefly, then
  resume into the planned slow first 150 m of the run.
- **Sled Pull in (18→8–11):** route to your lane directly (walk it Friday), chalk
  once and decisively, grip and go.
- **Burpees / Row / Farmers:** jog the simpler ones; the row's cost is the straps —
  drill a fixed strap-on and strap-off sequence (`hyrox-erg-strategy-coach` for the
  rowing technique itself).
- **Lunges out (19→12–15):** brief power-walk, ribs down, eyes up, resume.
- **Wall Balls in/out (11/8):** fine; jog in, chalk if you use it.

**Walk-vs-run map:** jog the SkiErg, Burpee, Row, Farmers, Wall-Ball transitions;
power-walk briefly out of the two sleds and the lunges. Not a blanket rule —
station-specific.

**Drills (make them automatic):**
- Strap drill: 10× strap-on/row-3-strokes/strap-off, timing each.
- Chalk-and-grip drill: approach → chalk → grip the implement, 10 reps, on the move.
- Sled-out drill: heavy sled effort → 5 s power-walk → resume to target — rehearses
  not dawdling while the legs are dead.

**Venue walkthrough Friday:** map the line to each station and lane; note where the
roxzone path is long so you don't take a wide line race day. Re-time every
transition at your next simulation and we'll re-tune the ranges.

**Boundary note:** the *runs and station times* themselves are the pacing planner's
job (`hyrox-race-pacing-planner`); I've only touched the roxzone.
