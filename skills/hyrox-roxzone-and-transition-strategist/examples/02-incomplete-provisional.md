# Example 02 — Incomplete data: no venue, no measured transitions

## Request

> My roxzones feel slow but I've never timed them and I don't know the venue yet.
> How do I make my transitions faster in HYROX?

## Response (abridged, PROVISIONAL)

This is a **provisional** plan. You haven't timed your transitions or seen the
venue, so I can't give you confident per-transition second-counts — roxzone size,
layout, and crowding vary by event, and a "standard" number asserted from memory
would be made up. The ranges below are **starting assumptions to measure**, not
truths.

**What's universally true and you can act on now:**
- The roxzone is **real, measurable time** across **16 transitions** (in and out of
  8 stations). Trimming even a handful of seconds each adds up to a meaningful chunk
  of your finish with **no extra fitness**.
- Don't slam to a walk the instant you cross the line, and don't sprint in cold —
  plan where you ease and where you resume.
- **Walk-vs-run is station-specific:** brief power-walk out of the sleds and lunges
  (the legs are most cooked), jog the cheaper transitions.
- **Setup is a skill:** clean erg entry, decisive chalk on grip stations (sled pull,
  farmers, wall balls), a practiced rower strap-on/strap-off sequence. Fumbling is
  pure lost time.

**Provisional target frame (assumptions — replace with your measured times):**
a clean, well-drilled simple station entry is often on the order of a handful of
seconds; transitions with straps, setup, or a long roxzone path run longer. I'm
deliberately not putting a precise number on each — that's what measuring is for.

**The two things that unlock the real plan:**
1. **Time your transitions** at your next simulation — every run-in and run-out
   separately. (Set this up via `hyrox-simulation-builder`.) Bring me the 16
   numbers.
2. **Walk the venue** once you know it: map the line and lane to each station so you
   don't hesitate or take a wide line.

Once you have measured transitions and venue notes, I'll turn this into a
transition-by-transition plan with your real numbers and the highest-value fixes
ranked. I'm not inventing those numbers in the meantime.
