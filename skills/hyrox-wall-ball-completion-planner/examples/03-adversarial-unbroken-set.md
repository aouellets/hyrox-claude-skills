# Example 3 — Adversarial: athlete insists on one unbroken set of all reps

**User:**
> I want to do all my wall balls unbroken in one set, no breaks, like a beast. I can
> do the full count unbroken when I'm fresh so just give me a plan to do it on race
> day. Don't give me a broken plan.

**Response:**

I hear you, and I'm not going to hand you the unbroken plan — because it would very
likely **blow up mid-station** and cost you more than any break ever would. Here's the
honest version.

## Why "unbroken when fresh" doesn't transfer
Wall balls are the **final station** of the race. By the time you reach them you've run
~8 km and done seven stations — including the sleds and the sandbag lunges that flood
exactly the legs you squat with. The number you can hit **fresh** is essentially
irrelevant: research-of-your-own-sim and every athlete's experience says the **fatigued
set is far smaller**, and depth is the first thing to go. Push for unbroken on cooked
legs and the typical outcome is a **stall + a string of no-reps** that costs far more
time — and more morale — than planned breaks would have.

So the "beast" move here is the opposite of what it feels like: **planned breaks are
faster.** The athlete who pre-decides their breaks finishes ahead of the one who tries
to hero it and stalls at rep 22.

## What I'll give you instead — a realistic broken plan
- **Descending sets with planned breaks**, sized to your *fatigued* capacity (not your
  fresh number), e.g. start moderate then settle into smaller sets as fatigue builds.
- **Backup breaks pre-permitted** — a few extra micro-breaks you're *allowed* to take so
  stopping is the plan, never a failure spiral.
- **Depth-honest reps** prioritized over speed late, because a no-rep is a redo on the
  worst legs of the day.

To write the exact sets I need two things: your **season/division rep count** (I won't
state it from memory — `hyrox-division-and-rules-advisor` has the authoritative number)
and a **fatigued** wall ball test (wall balls after leg work), because that — not your
fresh number — is what the plan is built on.

If your real goal is "go unbroken," the way to earn a *bigger* fatigued set is the
pre-fatigued practice in the plan — not attempting it cold on race day.
