# Example 1 — Complete data: broken-set completion plan on cooked legs

**User:**
> Plan my wall balls. My division this season is 100 reps. Fresh I can do 40 unbroken,
> but I've never tested them tired. In a sim last month after the lunges I got 18 then
> totally fell apart and kept getting no-repped for shallow depth. Race in 6 weeks.

**Response:**

Good — you've given me the most important thing most people skip: a **tired** data
point. That 18-then-fell-apart is your real planning number, not the fresh 40.

## Station framing & rep count
- Wall balls are the **final station**, on the most cooked legs of your day, no run
  after — the whole game is **finish without a stall**.
- **100 reps** as you stated it for this season/division. I'm taking that as given; if
  you want it confirmed authoritatively, check `hyrox-division-and-rules-advisor`.
  The plan below is built as **proportions**, so it holds even if that number changes.

## Capacity read & fatigued estimate
- Fresh: 40 unbroken. **Race-relevant: far lower.** Your sim showed ~18 before the
  wheels came off and depth went. So your fatigued first set is realistically
  **~12–15**, not 18 — we plan *under* the cliff, not at it. (Estimate, labeled.)

## Squat & throw efficiency
- The no-reps are your biggest leak: a shallow rep costs you a **redo on the worst legs
  of the day**. Bias **depth and target on every rep**, even if it feels slow.
- **Catch into the squat** — receive the ball straight into the descent, don't catch,
  pause, then squat. Drive the throw with the **legs**; when the legs are gone late,
  resist arm-muscling, just keep depth honest.

## Broken-set plan (for 100; proportional)
Planned **descending** sets with planned breaks — pre-decided so a break is the plan,
not a failure:
- **15 / 12 / 10 / 10 / 8 / 8 / 8 / 7 / 7 / 7 / 8** ≈ 100, with a short break (3–5
  controlled breaths) between each.
- **Backup breaks allowed:** if a set is cracking, stop one rep early and breathe — you
  may add up to ~3 extra micro-breaks total without it being a problem. Pre-permitting
  them is what stops the spiral.

## Breathing
Exhale on the throw. On each planned break: 3–5 deliberate exhale-focused breaths to
reset — don't stand there gassed and panicked.

## No-rep mitigation
Late in the station, **slow the cadence before you lose depth**. Standard-honest reps
at a steady rhythm finish faster than fast reps that get waved off and redone.

## Fatigue-specific practice & prep volume (6 weeks)
- 2×/week, rehearse wall balls **pre-fatigued**: e.g. sandbag lunges or a hard 400 m →
  straight into your broken-set plan. This is the only practice that matters here.
- **Progression criterion:** you hold the planned set sizes (15/12/10…) *under fatigue*
  with clean depth. When that's solid, nudge the first two sets up by 1–2 reps.
- **Retest:** week 4 — a pre-fatigued full 100 (or your confirmed count) for time and
  number of unplanned breaks. Win condition: fewer unplanned breaks, zero no-reps.

Keep the late-set fatigue as ordinary tired-legs burn; sharp or localized knee/shoulder
pain isn't part of the plan — back off and get it looked at if that shows up.
