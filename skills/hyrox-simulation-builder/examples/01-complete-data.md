# Example 01 — Complete data (run-station bricks chosen over a full sim)

**Request:**

> "I'm 9 weeks out from HYROX Singles, goal ~1:15. My fresh 1 km is ~4:45 and I
> have a full sled and rower at my gym. The post-sled runs always wreck me — I
> slow to ~6:00/km for the first 300 m. I want a session this week to fix that.
> Should I just do a full race sim?"

## Rehearsal goal & format chosen

**Goal:** improve compromised running off the sleds — the named limiter.
**Format: run-station bricks (sled-to-run focus).** This is the smallest
sufficient format. A full simulation is the wrong tool here: it would cost you a
week of recovery to rehearse one transition you can train far more cheaply, and at
9 weeks out you want the easy volume and quality running protected, not a benchmark
you haven't earned yet. Save the full sim for a sparse, deliberate point later in
the build.

## Session structure

4–5 bricks, each: **sled push** (race-effort, ~25–50 m depending on your gym
sled — labeled, see below) → **600 m run** at the target you can actually hold
fatigued. Walk-back recovery to the sled between bricks (~2–3 min). Substitution
note: **your gym sled on gym flooring is not race resistance** — treat the *effort*
as the constant, not the load number.

## Intensity

- Sled: repeatable race effort, RPE ~8 — hard but leaving the legs able to run,
  not emptied.
- Run: first 100–200 m deliberately **under** target (~5:30/km) to let turnover
  settle, then settle into ~5:00–5:10/km by 300 m. Resist chasing your ~4:45 fresh
  pace off the sled — that spike is exactly what's costing you.

## Data to collect

Per brick: sled effort/RPE, run pace for the first 200 m and for 200–600 m, how
many metres until cadence returns, grip/leg notes. Compare brick 1 to brick 5 to
see fade.

## Recovery cost & placement

Moderate. Put it as a key session mid-week with an easy run or rest the day after;
keep the surrounding easy days easy.

## Advancement criterion

When you hold ~5:10/km from 200 m onward across all 5 bricks with turnover back by
~200 m, progress: add a 6th brick **or** lengthen the run to 800 m — not both at
once.

## Assumptions & confidence

Assumed gym sled (substitution labeled), ~4:45 fresh / ~6:00 current post-sled
pace as reported. **Confidence: high** for the format choice; the exact paces are
yours to confirm in session one. Route the full plan to
`hyrox-training-plan-builder` and the transition technique drilling to
`hyrox-compromised-running-coach`.
