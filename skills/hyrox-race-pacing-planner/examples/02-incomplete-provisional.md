# Example 02 — Incomplete data: goal time only, no station splits

## Request

> First HYROX in 6 weeks, Singles Open. I want to finish around 1:20. My fresh
> 1 km is about 5:30. That's all I've got — I've never done the stations under
> fatigue. What pace should I run?

## Response (abridged, PROVISIONAL)

This is a **provisional** plan. You gave a goal and a fresh pace but no station
times and no compromised-run experience, so the station targets and the fade
ladder below are **labeled assumptions**, not measured numbers — I will not invent
precise station splits for a race you haven't rehearsed.

**Goal & assumptions.** Singles Open, first race, goal ~1:20, fresh 5:30/km.
Assumed (to be replaced by a simulation): a moderate first-timer fade, slow
roxzones, and conservative station efforts. **Confidence: low-medium** — the
biggest unknown is how your runs hold up after the sleds and lunges, which I have
no data on.

**What I can give you now:**

- **Effort, not pace, is your anchor.** As a first-timer, run **by RPE**: Runs 1–3
  at ~RPE 7 ("comfortably hard," short sentences only), and expect the pace to
  drift slower — that drift is normal and planned, not a failure.
- **A provisional run ladder** (bands, assumption-based):
  Runs 1–2 ~5:35–5:45/km, middle runs ~5:50–6:10, final runs ~6:10–6:40. These
  widen because I'm guessing your fade — treat them as a starting frame.
- **Station rule of thumb:** pace every station to leave your legs able to *run*
  the next 1 km, not to empty yourself. Break the wall balls into sets (e.g. 10s)
  from the start.
- **Roxzones:** don't slam to a walk crossing the line; jog through the simpler
  transitions, power-walk briefly after the sleds and lunges.

**Projected finish range:** roughly **1:18–1:28**, deliberately wide because the
station-under-fatigue data is missing. I'm not narrowing this without a simulation.

**The one thing that would sharpen all of this:** do one **half or full simulation**
in the next 2–3 weeks and bring me the station times and how your runs felt after
the sleds. Then I can replace every assumption above with your numbers and tighten
the bands. (Building that simulation is `hyrox-simulation-builder`.)

**What I'm deliberately not doing:** I'm not handing you "Run 1: 5:30, Run 2: 5:35…"
as if it were measured — that would be false precision for a first race I have no
station data on. The bands and the RPE anchor are the honest version.
