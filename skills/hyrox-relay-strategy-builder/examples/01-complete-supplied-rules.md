# Example 01 — Complete: rules supplied, four-member profiles, multiple assignments

**Request:**

> "Relay, 2026 season. Our event rule sheet says: *'the relay is split into 4 equal
> segments; each member completes 2 of the 8 run+station pairs in sequence, handing
> off in the transition box after their second station.'* Our four:
> - **Mara** — fastest runner (4:00/km), average stations.
> - **Tom** — sled monster (push & pull), runs ~4:40, fades on grip.
> - **Priya** — strong grip + engine (ski/row), runs ~4:25.
> - **Ben** — strong overhead/wall ball + burpees, runs ~4:30.
>
> Give us assignment options."

### Division & season in use

- **Relay, 2026 season.**

  Push, 3 Sled Pull, 4 Burpee Broad Jumps, 5 Row, 6 Farmers Carry, 7 Sandbag Lunges,

### Candidate assignments

**Assignment A — specialty-max**
- S1 Ski+SledPush → **Tom** (sled) — wait, sled push is here but ski isn't his; see tradeoff.
- S1 → **Priya** (ski engine), S2 SledPull+Burpees → **Tom** (pull) + Ben-ish burpees…

Let me lay it cleanly:

| Segment (pairs) | A | B | C |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1 Ski, Sled Push | Priya (ski) | Tom (push) | Priya |
| S2 Sled Pull, Burpees | Tom (pull) | Ben (burpees) | Tom |

### Tradeoff table

| | Maximizes | Risks (worst-case ill-fit) |
|---|---|---|
| **A** | Tom on sled **pull**, Ben on wall balls, Priya on ski | Puts **Tom's grip-fade** on sled pull (grip-heavy) right where he's weakest; Mara (your fastest runner) on a station-heavy S3 wastes her run edge |
| **B** | Tom on sled **push**, Priya's grip on farmers carry, Ben's burpees | Splits Tom's two sled strengths across non-adjacent segments — fine — but Mara ends on wall balls, not her strength |
| **C** | Priya ski, Tom both sleds adjacent (S1-S2 overlap not allowed here, so partial), Mara's run on the final running-exposed segment | Tom doing **both** sled-ish pairs back-to-back stacks posterior-chain cost; verify segment adjacency is legal |

### Recommended

**Assignment B**, moderately confident. It keeps Tom on his stronger sled (push),
uses Priya's grip where grip is taxed (farmers carry), gives Ben the burpees, and
puts Mara where her running matters most. It avoids A's mistake of handing Tom a
grip-heavy pull. The cost: Mara finishes on wall balls (not her strength) — if her
wall ball is poor, swap S4 to Ben and move Mara to S3.

**What would change it:** Tom's actual grip on sled pull, and Mara's wall-ball
number. Give me those and I'll firm up A vs B.

- Rehearse the **transition-box handoff after the second station** exactly as your
