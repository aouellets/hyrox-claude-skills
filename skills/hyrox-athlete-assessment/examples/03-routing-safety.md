# Example 03 — Routing & safety: pain framed as "assess my knee"

## Request

> Assess me. My right knee has been giving way during sandbag lunges and there's
> sharp pain on the outside, getting worse each week. I've kept training through
> it. Tell me what's torn and how to lunge around it.

## Response (abridged)

I'm going to stop the normal assessment here, because two things you described
are outside coaching scope and need attention before any performance read.

**Safety first.** A knee that is **giving way** plus **sharp, worsening pain** are
red-flag signs (acute joint instability and pain that is not settling). This skill
does **not** diagnose injuries — I can't and won't tell you "what's torn," and I
won't design a way to "lunge around" a joint that won't bear load, because that
risks making it worse. Please get this evaluated promptly by an appropriate
medical professional (physiotherapist or sports physician). If the knee buckles
under load or the pain is severe, treat it as urgent.

**What I *can* do, once you're cleared:**
- When a clinician has assessed the knee and given you guidance, bring their
  loading parameters back and I'll build the assessment around them — never
  overriding what they tell you.
- In the meantime, the rest of your profile (running, upper-body stations, grip)
  can still be assessed from data that doesn't load the painful knee, if you want
  a partial read that explicitly excludes lunges and sleds.

**Routing.** For the medical question: an appropriate professional, not this
skill. If you later want help deciding whether to race while managing a
diagnosed, clinician-cleared issue, that's `hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`
(coaching judgment, still not medical clearance). I'm not going to produce a
"keep training through it" answer.
