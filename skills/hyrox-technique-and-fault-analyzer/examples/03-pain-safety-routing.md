# Example 3 — Safety + routing: a "fault" that is actually pain

**User:**
> Analyze my sandbag lunge technique — every time my left knee caves in and there's
> a sharp pain on the inside of the knee that's been getting worse over the last
> two weeks, even walking now.

**Response:**

I'm going to stop the technique analysis here, because what you've described isn't a
movement fault to drill — it's **pain that is sharp, localized, and worsening**, now
present even when walking. That is outside what coaching should work around.

## This needs evaluation, not a cue
Sharp inside-knee pain with the knee caving, **worsening over two weeks and now
hurting during normal walking**, is a red flag per
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md). Please get it
assessed by an appropriate medical professional (e.g. a physiotherapist or
physician) before loading it further. I won't prescribe lunge drills or "fix the
knee cave" cues over the top of a worsening, painful joint — doing that could make
it worse, and I can't tell from a description whether this is a correctable
movement habit or something that needs treatment.

## What I can and can't separate
- **Observation:** knee caves inward on the left under load, with sharp medial knee
  pain, worsening, now present walking.
- **What I cannot determine:** whether the cave is causing the pain, the pain is
  causing the cave, or there's an underlying issue — and I haven't seen it. That
  uncertainty is exactly why this goes to a clinician, not a drill.

## After you're cleared
If a professional clears you and it turns out to be a *load-tolerant* movement habit
(knee tracking under fatigue), come back and I'll do the proper fault analysis —
knee tracking is a known sandbag-lunge limiter — and then route you to
`hyrox-station-progression-builder` to rebuild the lunge load gradually. But the
clearance comes first.
