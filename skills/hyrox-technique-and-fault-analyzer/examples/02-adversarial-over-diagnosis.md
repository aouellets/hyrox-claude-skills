# Example 2 — Adversarial: a vague one-liner demanding a definitive diagnosis

**User:**
> My wall balls are slow. Just tell me the one thing that's wrong so I can fix it.
> Don't give me a list — what is THE fault?

**Response:**

I can't honestly name a single definite fault from "wall balls are slow" — and
giving you a confident one-word answer would be guessing dressed up as coaching.
"Slow" has several common causes that need *different* fixes, and picking one
blind would likely send you to drill the wrong thing. Here's the disciplined
version, then the one or two questions that would let me actually narrow it.

## Observations (what you reported)
- Wall balls feel **slow**. That's it — no detail on depth, target, breaks, or when
  it happens.

## What cannot be determined from this
- Whether you're missing depth or target standards (no-reps that *look* like
  slowness).
- Whether it's **pacing** (going to failure and resting a lot) vs **mechanics**
  (inefficient catch-to-squat) vs **fatigue** (legs cooked from the rest of the
  race, since this is the last station).
- Whether it's even truly slow or just *feels* slow.
- I have not seen the movement.

## Candidate faults (ranked by time cost) — none confirmed yet
| Candidate | Confirming sign | Time cost | No-rep risk | Correction ease |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|
| Breaking into small sets / going to failure (pacing) | Long mid-set rests | **High** | Low | Moderate |
| Missing depth or target (no-reps) | Reps not counting | High | **High** | Easy–moderate |
| Inefficient catch-into-squat rhythm | Pausing between catch and descent | Med | Low | Moderate |
| Legs cooked from the race (it's the last station) | Only slow in a full race, fine fresh | Med-high | Low | (pacing/conditioning, not technique) |

## The few discriminating questions (this is what I need)
1. **Fresh or only at the end of a race?** (Fatigue/pacing vs mechanics.)
2. **Are reps getting no-repped** for depth or target?
3. **Do you break into small sets and rest, or grind continuously and just feel slow?**

Answer those three and I'll give you the actual top fault with a cue and a drill —
or, if it turns out to be pacing/fatigue rather than a technique fault, route you to
`hyrox-station-progression-builder` (fatigued-leg repeatability) or
`hyrox-wall-ball-completion-planner` (break strategy). I'd rather get you the right
fix than a fast wrong one.
