# Example 1 — Complete-enough data: a detailed sled-push observation

**User:**
> On the HYROX sled push I start fine but about halfway I stall and have to do
> these little stop-start surges to get it moving again. I notice when I look at a
> video that my hips come up and I'm pretty upright by the second half, arms
> bending. It's my legs that feel done, not my grip. Happens worse on the second
> length back.

**Response:**

This description is detailed (you've watched video and isolated *legs, not grip*),
so I can give a confident read — but I'll still mark where I'm inferring.

## Observations (what you reported)
- Strong start, then a **mid-effort stall** with stop-start surges to re-initiate.
- On video: **hips rising and torso becoming upright** in the second half.
- **Arms bending** later in the push.
- Sensation is **legs**, not grip.
- Worse on the **return length**.

*Note: I'm reading your description of your own video, not the video itself.*

## What cannot be determined
- Your exact body angle/foot position numerically, or hand height on the uprights.
- Whether the surface/sled friction is contributing (gym sled feel ≠ race
  resistance — surface and sled model change this a lot).
- Whether the early pace was *too hot*, causing the stall, vs a pure mechanics fault.

## Prioritized faults table (by time cost)
| Candidate fault | Confirming sign | Time cost | Energy cost | No-rep risk | Safety | Correction ease |
|-----------------|-----------------|-----------|-------------|-------------|--------|-----------------|
| **Posture breakdown (hips rise, torso upright)** → losing horizontal force | The upright second half you saw | **High** | High | n/a | Low-mod | **Easy–moderate** |
| **Stop-start instead of continuous drive** | Surging to re-initiate inertia repeatedly | High | **Very high** | n/a | Low | Moderate |
| **Going out too hot** (pacing, not mechanics) | Stall lines up with a fast start | Med–high | High | n/a | Low | Easy |
| **Arms bending** (bleak of leg drive into arms) | Elbows flexing late | Med | Med | n/a | Low | Easy |

**Fix first:** the **posture breakdown** — it's the highest time cost and among the
easiest to correct, and the upright torso is exactly what kills horizontal force.

## Discriminating check (resolves pacing vs mechanics)
- Do one push at ~80% of your usual starting pace. If you *don't* stall, pacing is a
  big contributor; if you still stall with the upright torso, it's primarily
  mechanics.

## Cues (conditional on the above)
- "Long line hands-to-heels, hips down" — keep the low body angle through the back half.
- "Continuous drive, no surges" — choppy powerful steps, never let it fully stop.
- "Drive the floor away, arms are struts" — stop pulling with bent arms.

## Drills
- Short heavy sled holds at the low angle (groove the posture under load).
- 4×(20–25 m continuous drive) at moderate load, focus on *no stop-start*.
