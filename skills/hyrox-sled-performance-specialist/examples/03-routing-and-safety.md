# Example 3 — Routing + safety: no sled, plus a non-sled station, plus a red flag

**User:**
> My gym just lost its sled. How do I keep training the sled push? Also build my
> wall balls while you're at it. And — last heavy sled session left me with one
> calf that's really swollen, hot, and tender, and it's getting worse, not better,
> three days later.

**Response:**

Three different things here; I'll take the health one first because it changes
everything else.

### Safety first — this calf needs evaluation
A single calf that is **swollen, hot, tender, and getting worse three days later**
is not ordinary DOMS, and it's outside coaching scope to train around. Please get it
assessed by a medical professional promptly — localized warmth and swelling that's
worsening rather than settling is a red flag
([safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)). I won't prescribe
sled or wall-ball loading on top of it. Hold the loaded work until you're cleared.

### "No sled" — that's a substitution question, not a sled-coaching one
When you have **no sled at all**, I'm not the right tool to improvise a swap — I
coach the sled work proper. Route that to:

→ **`hyrox-equipment-substitution-engine`**, which designs substitutions (e.g.
heavy resisted variations, leg-drive work) that approximate the demand. Important
even there: **a substitute's load won't equal the race sled's resistance** — turf,
flooring, sled model, friction, and calibration all change race feel, so any swap
is an approximation, not an equivalent. Once you have access to a real sled again,
come back and I'll coach the push mechanics and repeatability.

### "Build my wall balls" — different station, different skill
Wall balls aren't a sled station, so that's not mine either:

→ **`hyrox-station-progression-builder`** owns the other six stations (including
wall balls). I only handle the two sleds (and I'll sequence sled work into a block
when asked).

So: clear the calf first, then substitution engine for the no-sled push, and the
progression builder for wall balls. I've deliberately not prescribed loaded work
given the symptom.
