## Pull coaching (fully actionable now)
Your two problems — **grip failure** and **rope tangling** — are the two classic
pull limiters, and they compound each other.

- **Pulling posture:** braced, anchored, low — hips back, trunk supported, so legs
  and back do the work, not just the arms and grip. Pulling tall burns the grip.
- **Rope management:** this is a *rehearsed skill*, not luck. Pull hand-over-hand
  and **stack/feed the slack cleanly to one side** so it never piles into a tangle.
  Practice this slow and deliberate before adding speed.
- **Hand-over-hand rhythm:** smooth, continuous, moderate-size pulls beat big jerky
  yanks — rhythm preserves grip and keeps the sled moving.
- **Grip endurance:** build it with loaded carries, dead hangs, and the pull itself;
  the goal is grip that lasts the full distance without a reset.

## Provisional conditioning (baseline first)
- **Week 1 baseline (your surface):** one pull for time + how far before grip
  fails; 3× shorter pulls focusing on clean rope feed.
- Then: 2×/week — one grip-endurance day (carries + hangs + holds) and one pull
  repeatability day (short race-effort pulls + a 200 m run), progressing as grip
  lasts longer. Loads filled in once you give me the surface and baseline.

## Retest
Repeat the Week-1 baseline in ~4 weeks: target further before grip failure and a
clean, untangled rope every rep.
