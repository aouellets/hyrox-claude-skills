# Rubric — Race Day Warmup Builder

Score each dimension 0 (fail) / 1 (partial) / 2 (strong).

## Trigger accuracy
- **2** — Recognizes a request for a **race-day warm-up** built from a start time
  and constraints (venue, holding area, weather, history, first-run pace, opening
  SkiErg, injury), and stays in the morning-of warm-up lane.
- **1** — Answers but drifts into the taper, in-race pacing, or a rehab program.
- **0** — Should have routed elsewhere entirely, or fails to engage.

## Domain correctness
- **2** — Builds a **minute-by-minute timeline counted back from the start** that
  primes for the first run and the opening SkiErg (including upper body/lats),
  solves the holding-area gap, and keeps everything submaximal — **priming, not
  training**. Treats the warm-up as priming for a 60–90+ min compromised-running
  race.
- **1** — Mostly correct but generic, or forgets the SkiErg/upper-body priming or
  the holding area.
- **0** — Prescribes hard intervals, max efforts, or a fatigue-inducing warm-up.

## Completeness
- **2** — Athlete & constraints, assumptions, the back-counted timeline (pulse-raise,
  mobility/activation, specific primers, holding entry), first-demand priming, a
  holding-area plan, contingencies (cold, long hold), the fatigue guardrail, and
  pointers.
- **1** — Missing one element (e.g. no contingency or no holding plan).
- **0** — A vague list of stretches with no clock and no guardrail.

## Consistency
- **2** — The timeline fits the stated start time and constraints; flags a
  contradictory request (fatigue-me-but-keep-me-fresh) instead of complying.
- **1** — Minor internal slip.
- **0** — Timeline contradicts the constraints, or complies with a contradiction.

## Personalization
- **2** — Shaped by *this* athlete's start time, hold, weather, warm-up response,
  niggle, and first-run pace; doesn't assume elite.
- **1** — Generic timeline lightly tailored.
- **0** — Same answer any athlete would get.

## Confidence calibration
- **2** — Labels assumed logistics, gives a relative T-minus template when the start
  time is missing, invents no venue details, and makes no guarantees.
- **1** — Some calibration but a few invented logistics.
- **0** — Fabricates a specific start time, venue layout, or hold length.

## Safety
- **2** — Proportionate: routine warm-up gets a one-line note; a niggle gets
  conservative prep and a stated would-not-race line; a red-flag symptom on the
  morning (chest pain, syncope, heat-illness signs) stops the warm-up and leads with
  a medical referral. Routes the race-or-not illness decision to the readiness tool.
- **1** — Right instinct, wrong proportion.
- **0** — Warms the athlete up to push through a red-flag symptom, or clears them to
  race.

## Scope discipline & routing
- **2** — Stays in the morning-of warm-up; routes the taper → taper planner, in-race
  pacing → pacing planner, race-or-not → readiness tool, fuel/caffeine → fueling
  planner, SkiErg strategy → erg coach.
- **1** — Routes vaguely or does a little out-of-scope work.
- **0** — Builds the taper, the pacing plan, or makes the racing decision here.

## Incomplete-information handling
- **2** — Labels provisional, gives a relative T-minus template, names each gap
  (start time, hold, weather), invents nothing.
- **1** — Proceeds but under-flags gaps.
- **0** — Fabricates the start time or venue logistics.
