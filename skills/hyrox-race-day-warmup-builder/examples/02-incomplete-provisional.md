# Example 02 — Incomplete data: no start time, no venue details

## Request

> Can you give me a HYROX race-day warm-up? I don't know my exact wave time yet and
> I haven't been to the venue.

## Response (abridged, PROVISIONAL)

This is a **provisional** warm-up. You don't have your start time or the venue/
holding details yet, and those are exactly what shape the back half of a warm-up —
so I'll give you a **relative T-minus template** you can drop a real clock onto, and
flag the assumptions. I'm not inventing your venue's logistics.

**Assumptions (replace when you know them):** a moderate hold (~10 min penned) and
mild/indoor conditions. If it's cold, or the hold is long, the timeline changes —
contingencies below.

**Relative template (count back from your gun once you have it):**

| Clock | Block | Do |
|---|---|---|
| T-40 | Stay warm / arrive | Easy walking, layers if cold |
| T-32 | Pulse-raise | 6–8 min easy jog to conversational |
| T-24 | Mobility / activation | Ankles, hips, T-spine, lats & shoulders (for the opening SkiErg) |
| T-16 | Specific primers | 3–4 × 20 s strides at your first-run effort; short easy SkiErg if one's available |
| T-12 | Into holding | Layer up; enter the pen |
| T-12 → gun | Holding | Stay warm; small re-activations if there's room; drop the last layer in the final ~minute |
| Gun | Start | Controlled first run, not fast |

**The fatigue guardrail still applies:** priming, not training. Nothing maximal,
nothing that spends the race.

**Three things that turn this into a real plan:**
1. **Your start time** — so I can put real clock times on every block.
2. **The holding-area situation** — how long penned and how much room (this drives
   the late timing and the re-warm plan more than anything).
3. **Weather** — cold means warming later and re-warming in the pen; heat means
   shortening it.

Tell me those three (plus your first-run target pace if you have it) and I'll
convert this template into a minute-by-minute timeline. Until then I'm deliberately
not inventing a 09:00-anything or a specific venue's pen.
