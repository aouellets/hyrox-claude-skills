# Example 01 — Complete data: 09:40 start, long cold hold, slow to warm up

## Request

> My wave starts 09:40, check-in 09:00. It's an indoor venue but check-in is a cold
> outdoor marquee and they pen us ~12 min before the gun with no room to move.
> ~6°C outside. I'm 41 and slow to warm up. First run target ~5:15/km. Mild left
> Achilles I baby in warm-ups but it's fine to race. There's a warm-up area with a
> SkiErg. Build my warm-up.

## Response (abridged)

**Athlete & constraints.** Start 09:40; long, **cold (~6°C)** outdoor hold for
~12 min in a no-move pen; slow to warm up at 41; first run ~5:15/km; mild left
Achilles to prep gently. Warm-up SkiErg available. **Confidence: high** — start
time, hold, weather, and first-run pace all given. The cold pen is the design
driver: I'll warm you *later* and keep you warm into the gun.

**Minute-by-minute (counted back from 09:40 gun):**

| Clock | Block | Do | Intensity |
|---|---|---|---|
| T-40 (09:00) | Check-in / stay warm | Layers on; easy walking; don't cool down | very low |
| T-32 (09:08) | General pulse-raise | 6–8 min easy jog, build to conversational | low |
| T-24 (09:16) | Mobility / activation | Ankles (gentle for the Achilles), hips, T-spine, lats/shoulders for the ski; calf raises *easy* range only | low |
| T-16 (09:24) | Specific primers | 3–4 × 20 s strides at ~5:15/km first-run effort, full recovery; then 200–300 m easy SkiErg to wake the pattern, not to fatigue | moderate, brief |
| T-12 (09:28) | Into the pen | Final layer ON over warm muscles; enter holding | — |
| T-12→0 | Holding (no-move pen) | Stay warm: layered, small heel raises / calf pumps / shoulder rolls if any room; mental rehearsal of the first run + ski; final layer off in the last ~60–90 s | minimal |
| 09:40 | Gun | Controlled first run at ~5:15/km, NOT faster | — |

**First-demand priming.** The strides match your 5:15 first-run pace so you don't
go out hot; the short easy ski wakes the upper-body/lat pattern for the opening
SkiErg without spending anything. The Achilles gets easy-range calf work only — no
loaded end-range, no plyo.

**Cold-pen plan (your key risk).** Because you're slow to warm up *and* will sit
cold for 12 min, the primers are timed **late** (finishing ~T-12) and you keep a
layer on into the pen. The danger is arriving at the gun cold and stiff — the heel
raises and shoulder rolls in the pen, plus dropping the last layer only in the final
~minute, bridge that gap.

**Contingencies.**
- **If the hold runs longer than 12 min:** repeat 4–5 easy heel raises and shoulder
  rolls every few minutes; do *not* re-run hard — keep it minimal re-activation.
- **Colder than forecast / wind:** add a second easy jog block at T-20, keep both
  layers until later.

**Fatigue guardrail.** This is **priming, not training** — nothing maximal, no long
intervals, no testing your legs. You should feel switched-on and warm at the gun,
not worked. Total hard time is a few strides and a short easy ski; that's it.
