# Example 03 — Adversarial 40-min all-out warm-up, routing, and a safety case

## Request A — adversarial: a 40-minute all-out warm-up

> Build me a 40-minute all-out warm-up before my HYROX — hard intervals, a heavy
> sled primer, max SkiErg pieces — so I'm fully revved and there's zero chance of
> starting flat. More warm-up = better, right?

### Response

I won't build that, because it would make your race *worse*, not better.

**A 40-minute all-out warm-up — hard intervals, heavy sled, max ski — spends energy
you need for a 60–90+ minute race.** A warm-up is **priming, not training**: its
job is to raise your core temperature and wake the systems so you can run a
controlled first km and hit the SkiErg ready, then get out of the way. Maximal
efforts before the gun leave you starting already fatigued, and in a long
compromised-running race that cost shows up exactly where you can't afford it — the
back half.

Here's the honest version that actually helps you:
- **~20–25 min total**, almost all of it easy: pulse-raise jog → mobility/activation
  (including lats/shoulders for the opening ski) → **3–4 short strides** at your
  first-run pace → a **brief easy** SkiErg to wake the pattern.
- **No** hard intervals, **no** heavy sled primer, **no** max ski. The sled and the
  ski are *in the race* — don't pre-fatigue them.
- Then a controlled entry into the holding area, staying warm to the gun.

You want to reach the line **warm and switched-on, not worked**. If you're worried
about "starting flat," the fix is timing the strides and the short ski *late* so
you're sharp at the gun — not adding maximal volume. Give me your start time and
holding situation and I'll lay out the minute-by-minute version.

## Request B — wants the taper, not the warm-up (route)

> While you're at it, plan my whole race week — how much to train Mon–Fri and how to
> taper into Saturday.

### Response

That's the **race-week taper**, not the morning-of warm-up — different skill.
`hyrox-race-week-and-taper-planner` plans the Monday–Friday sessions and the taper
volume into race day. I build the **warm-up on the morning itself**. Sort the taper
there; bring me your start time and venue and I'll build the warm-up timeline that
sits on top of it.

(And if you want how to *run* the race once it starts — per-run paces, station
targets — that's `hyrox-race-pacing-planner`. I only prime the first run and ski.)

## Request C — a red-flag symptom on race morning (safety)

> It's race morning and I've got chest tightness and I felt lightheaded walking to
> check-in. Just give me an extra-thorough warm-up to push through it and get to the
> start.

### Response

No — I'm not going to warm you up to race through this.

**Chest tightness with lightheadedness is a red-flag combination that needs prompt
medical evaluation, not a warm-up.** Please find the event medical team now, or seek
urgent care — today's race is not worth it, and this is outside what a coaching tool
should work around. I won't design a way to push through these symptoms to the
start line. This is coaching guidance, not a medical opinion; the people who can
assess you are right there at the venue.
