# Example 03 — Routing + safety: simulation overload and a calf symptom

## Request A — wants a sim every session (route + guardrail)

> Just make every one of my sessions a full 8-station HYROX simulation so I get
> really good at running tired. More race reps = better, right?

### Response

Running tired is exactly what we want to train — but **not by simulating the whole
race every session.** A full 8-station simulation is the single most fatiguing
session you do and is costly to recover from; built too often it crowds out the
easy volume and quality running that actually build the engine, and you'd burn
down without a proportionate adaptation. That's the opposite of specificity done
well.

Compromised running is trained mostly with **bricks and segments** — a station
immediately followed by a run, biased to sled-to-run and lunge-to-run — with full
simulations reserved as **sparse, deliberate benchmark points**. So: 2 brick
sessions a week here, and the occasional full simulation routed to
`hyrox-simulation-builder` as a periodic test, not a daily diet. Tell me which
stations fade your runs worst and your compromised pace and I'll build the brick
progression.

## Request B — a niggle that crosses into a red flag (safety)

> Training the sled-to-run bricks. After the heavy sled pushes my right calf has a
> sharp, localized pain that's getting worse each session, and this morning the
> muscle is really swollen and tender out of proportion and my pee looked dark.
> How do I keep doing the bricks around it?

### Response

I need to stop the programming here and flag this.

**A sharp, localized, worsening calf pain combined with a muscle that is swollen
and tender out of proportion to the work, plus dark urine after heavy eccentric
sled work, are warning signs that need prompt medical evaluation** — that
combination (severe disproportionate muscle pain/swelling, weakness, dark/cola
urine after unaccustomed heavy eccentric loading) is the pattern associated with
rhabdomyolysis, which is a medical emergency, not a coaching problem. Please seek
urgent medical care rather than training around it.

I will not give you a way to keep doing the bricks through this. This is outside
coaching scope — the assessment and any clearance are a clinician's call. Once
you've been evaluated and cleared, I'll rebuild the compromised-running work, and
we'd reintroduce heavy eccentric sled volume very gradually with that history in
mind. This is coaching guidance, not a medical opinion.
