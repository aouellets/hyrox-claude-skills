# Example 01 — Complete data: sled-to-run is the worst fade

## Request

> I lose the most time on the runs after Sled Push and Sled Pull — my legs are
> bricks and I basically jog the first 200 m. Sandbag lunges hit me too. Fresh
> 1 km is 4:40; after the sleds my first 200 m is more like 5:45/km pace before I
> settle to ~5:10. 9 weeks out, Singles. I have a sled, a sandbag, and a
> treadmill. Train this.

## Response (abridged)

**Athlete & focus.** Singles, 9 weeks out. Worst fades: **sled-push → run,
sled-pull → run, lunge → run** (your stated leak points). Fresh 1 km 4:40;
compromised first-200 m ~5:45 settling to ~5:10. **Confidence: high** — you gave
fresh and compromised paces and the exact stations.

**Compromised pace targets (bands, from your data):**

| Transition | First 200 m target | Settle by 200–300 m |
|---|---|---|
| Sled push → run | 5:20–5:35/km (don't chase 4:40) | ~5:05/km |
| Sled pull → run | 5:20–5:35/km | ~5:05/km |
| Lunge → run | 5:15–5:30/km | ~5:00/km |

The point: your current 5:45 first-200 m is you fighting the deadness. Planned
slightly *under* target off the sled costs less than spiking and paying later.

**Transition sequence (drill the first 200 m every rep):**
1. **Pace control** — start a touch under target; let the system settle.
2. **Cadence restoration** — short, light, quick steps; turnover returns before
   stride length does. Aim to recover your fresh cadence within ~30 s.
3. **Breathing** — imposed exhale-focused rhythm, not panicked shallow breaths.
4. **Posture** — ribs down, eyes up, run tall to undo the sled-flexed position.

**Brick prescriptions (2 brick sessions/week, biased to sleds):**

- **Session A — sled-to-run (highest value).**
  4 × [sled push at race-ish effort ~15–20 m + 500 m run @ 5:10 target], walk-back
  rest ~2 min. Progression: hold 5:10 and recover cadence inside 200 m → add a 5th
  rep → extend run to 600 m → reduce rest.
- **Session B — mixed transitions.**
  3 rounds: [sled pull 15 m + 400 m @ 5:15] then [lunge 50 m + 400 m @ 5:10],
  90 s between movements. Vary the order week to week.

**Progressive fatigue ladder.** Wk 1–2: the two sessions above as written.
Wk 3–5: +1 rep on Session A, run segment 500→600 m. Wk 6–8: add a third short
transition to Session B. **Guardrail:** this is bricks and segments, *not* a full
8-station simulation — those stay sparse (route to `hyrox-simulation-builder`).
Advance only when the criterion is met, never on the calendar.

**Retests.** Wk 4 and wk 8: fixed sled-push effort → 400 m, measure (a) seconds to
recover fresh cadence and (b) average pace. Target: cadence back <25 s, pace held
at 5:05.

**Integration.** Put brick days away from your hardest standalone runs and heavy
lift day; keep easy volume genuinely easy on the days between. For the aerobic
engine itself, route to `hyrox-running-development-plan`.
