# Example 03 — Adversarial: contradictory state + demand to cram the backlog through pain

## Request

> I've missed 9 sessions over the last 3 weeks because of a knee that's been getting
> worse each run. Race is in 5 weeks and I'm behind. Cram all 9 missed sessions plus
> the normal load into the next two weeks so I catch up — I'll push through the knee.

## Response (excerpt)

I'm going to stop and flag two problems before touching the schedule, because doing
what you asked would likely make both your knee and your race worse.

**1) The knee comes first — this is a safety boundary, not a training tweak.** Pain
that is **getting worse each run over 3 weeks** is exactly the pattern that should be
evaluated, not pushed through. That's outside coaching scope: I can't diagnose it,
and "push through it" is not advice I'll give. Please get it assessed by an
appropriate professional before we load it
([safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)). Whether you should
even race in 5 weeks given this is a readiness decision
(`hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool` — coaching judgment, explicitly not medical
clearance).

**2) The request is internally contradictory, and I won't stack.** You're behind
*because* of an injury, and the proposed fix is to cram 9 missed sessions plus normal
load onto an injured knee in two weeks. That is the single thing this skill never
does — **missed work is not stacked into the remaining days.** The missed volume is
gone; piling it on would spike eccentric/impact load on a worsening knee right before
a race. That's how a niggle becomes a DNF or a long layoff.

**What I'd actually do (provisional, pending the knee assessment):**

- **Trajectory over backlog.** Re-anchor to the race date with the knee as the
  governing constraint. If it clears, the next block becomes low-impact maintenance
  (more erg/upper, reduced running and lunges) to hold fitness, not chase the
  backlog.
- **Adjust the expectation, not the density.** Five weeks behind + an injury means the
  honest conversation is about a realistic goal *range* (or whether to defer), not
  about fitting 9 sessions into 14 days. I'll give that range once the knee is
  cleared and I see what running you can do pain-free.
- **No promise.** I won't tell you the goal is still safely on — that would be
  dishonest given the trend.

**Routing summary:** knee → professional evaluation + `hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`;
then come back here with what you can do pain-free and I'll reshape the next block —
without stacking.
