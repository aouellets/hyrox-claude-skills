# Example 01 — Complete data: a missed week, revise the next block without stacking

## Request

> I'm in week 8 of a 16-week singles plan (race in 8 weeks, goal sub-1:10). I was
> sick last week and missed all 5 sessions. The two weeks before that I hit
> everything: threshold at 4:45/km felt on, sled bricks were solid. I'm recovered
> now — no symptoms, energy back. How do I adjust the next block? I'm tempted to
> double up to catch the work I lost.

## Response (excerpt)

**Current state vs. plan.** You're at the start of week 9 (capacity → race-specific
transition). Plan expected ~8 weeks of accumulated capacity work; you have ~7 — one
illness week missed cleanly, no symptoms now. The two weeks prior were on target
(threshold 4:45/km, bricks solid), so your fitness base is intact; you've lost a
little freshness/sharpness, not a phase.

**What changed and why.** This is a **short interruption**, not a fitness collapse.
The revision principle: re-enter at slightly reduced load for 2–3 sessions, then
resume the planned trajectory. **I am not stacking last week's 5 sessions onto this
week** — that missed volume is gone, and cramming it would spike your load right as
we move into the most demanding phase, costing you more than the week did.

**Revised next block (week 9 — re-entry, marked vs. original):**

- Mon — Easy 35 min @ 5:30 + strides ×4. *(unchanged)*
- Tue — Threshold: **4 × 5 min @ 4:45** *(original was 6 × 5; trimmed one round to
  re-enter intensity gently after illness)*.
- Wed — Strength/station at **RPE 7, not 8** *(de-load one notch; resume RPE 8 wk 10)*.
- Sat — Brick: **3 × (1 km @ 4:50 → sled 15 m → 200 m)** *(original was 4 rounds;
  one fewer this week only)*.
- Then **week 10 resumes the original plan in full** — no carryover.

**Progression decisions.** Running: hold pace, hold volume (don't chase). Threshold:
hold this week, advance to 6 × 5 in week 10 if Tue is clean. Stations: regress one
RPE notch this week, then advance as planned.

**Trajectory check.** The race date and the sub-1:10 *target range* still hold — one
clean illness week mid-block rarely moves a 16-week plan if you don't overcompensate.
Honest range stays roughly **1:09–1:12** depending on the race-specific block. No
promise of a number.

**Recovery & adjustment rules.** Keep easy days easy this week especially. If fatigue
lingers past 3 sessions, that's accumulating under-recovery, not re-entry — come back
and we hold longer. Next down week stays at week 13 as planned.

**Risks & referral.** You report no symptoms, so this is straightforward re-entry
(one-line scope note). If anything from the illness recurs — breathlessness, chest
tightness, lingering fatigue — that's a readiness/medical question, not a training
one ([safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md);
`hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`).
