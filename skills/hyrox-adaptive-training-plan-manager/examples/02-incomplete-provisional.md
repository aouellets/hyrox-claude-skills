# Example 02 — Incomplete data: provisional revision with a targeted ask

## Request

> Things have been rough lately and I've fallen behind on my HYROX plan. Can you
> adjust it?

## Response (excerpt)

I can revise your next block now on a **provisional** basis, and two answers will let
me lock it. I won't reconstruct a plan I can't see — I'll say what I'm assuming.

**What materially changes the revision (please confirm):**

1. **Race date / current phase** — how many weeks until the race, and roughly what
   block were you in (base, capacity, race-specific, taper)?
2. **What you actually did vs. missed in the last ~2 weeks** — which sessions
   happened, which didn't, and any fatigue, soreness, or pain.

I do **not** need your full original plan written out — the recent log plus the race
date is enough to reshape the next block.

**Provisional revision (assumed: ~6 weeks out, capacity phase, ~50% of sessions
missed over 2 weeks, no pain — all flagged):**

- **Principle:** preserve the trajectory, **do not stack** the missed sessions. We
  re-enter at reduced load and protect the key adaptation, not the backlog.
- **Next 1–2 weeks:** rebuild rhythm with easy running frequency + one quality
  session at reduced volume; hold station load one RPE notch down until you string
  together a clean week.
- **Expectation:** if the disruption is short, the original goal *range* likely still
  holds; if it's ongoing, we adjust the goal expectation rather than the daily
  density — I'll tell you which once I see the log.

**Assumptions flagged:** weeks out, phase, the amount missed, and absence of pain are
all assumed. If "rough lately" includes illness, persistent pain, or not eating/
fueling enough, that's a readiness/safety conversation first
(`hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`;
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md)), and I'd want to know
before prescribing load.
