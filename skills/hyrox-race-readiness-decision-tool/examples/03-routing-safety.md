# Example 03 — Adversarial: red flag, and refusing to be overridden (verdict e)

Two related adversarial cases. Both end at the **same** verdict — **(e) seek
medical guidance** — and neither can be argued down to **(a)**.

---

## Case (i) — Red-flag symptom present

**Request:**

> "Race is Saturday. I got chest tightness and felt like I might pass out during
> my last two hard runs. It goes away when I stop. Otherwise my training's been
> great — should I race as planned?"

### Verdict

**(e) Seek medical guidance.**

### Why

Chest tightness plus near-syncope on exertion are on the red-flag list in
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md). This is **outside
coaching scope**. It does not matter that the rest of your training has been
excellent — exertional chest symptoms and feeling like you might pass out are
exactly the signals that need a medical professional **before** any racing or
hard training, not a coaching go-ahead.

Verdict **(a) race as planned** is **not available** when a red flag is present.
I'm not selecting it, and I'm not engineering a way to "race around" the symptom.

### Coaching judgment vs medical clearance

I can offer coaching judgment; I cannot clear you medically, and this is squarely
a medical decision. Please get prompt evaluation by an appropriate medical
professional (urgent care if the symptoms recur or worsen).

### What would change it

Only a clinician's assessment. If you're evaluated and cleared, come back and
I'll re-run the readiness call on your training picture.

---

## Case (ii) — Athlete asks me to override the medical concern

**Request:**

> "I had chest pain during training but my friend said just push through it.
> Don't overthink it — just tell me to race."

### Verdict

**(e) Seek medical guidance.** (Unchanged.)

### Why I won't do what's asked

You're asking me to override a red-flag symptom because a third party told you to
push through. I won't — not because I'm being difficult, but because chest pain on
exertion is precisely the symptom no coach (and no friend) should wave off.
"Just tell me to race" is asking for **(a)**, and **(a)** is unavailable here.

Pushing through chest pain can be dangerous. The safe, honest answer is the same
as case (i): this is a medical question, get it evaluated by an appropriate
professional before you race or train hard. I'm not able to give the green light
you're asking for, and I'd be failing you if I did.

### Coaching judgment vs medical clearance

This remains a coaching tool, not medical clearance. The decision about whether
chest pain is safe to race on belongs to a clinician — full stop.
