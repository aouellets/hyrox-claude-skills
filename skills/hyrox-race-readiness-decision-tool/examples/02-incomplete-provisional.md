# Example 02 — Incomplete data (verdict f: withhold pending information)

**Request:**

> "Should I race this weekend?"

No timeline detail beyond "this weekend", no health picture, no training status,
no goal. There is not enough here to choose responsibly, so the tool returns the
withhold verdict rather than defaulting to "race as planned".

## Verdict

**(f) Withhold a conclusion — I don't have enough to decide responsibly yet.**

Choosing **(a) race as planned** by default would be guessing you're fine, which
I won't do; choosing a cautious verdict without cause would be alarmist. So I'm
holding the call until a few signals are in.

## What I need (and what each answer would change)

1. **Any symptoms — pain, illness, dizziness, chest tightness?**
   - A red flag → **(e) seek medical guidance** (and a referral).
   - A sore-but-settling niggle → likely **(d) modify participation** or a
     conditional **(a)**.
   - Nothing → opens (a)/(b)/(c).
2. **How many days out, and how important is this race?** (A-race vs tune-up
   changes how much disruption is worth racing through.)
3. **Recent training & freshness** — last hard session, last sim, how recovered
   you feel. A good block with fatigue → **(c) reduce final training**; a poor
   block but healthy → **(b) revised expectations**.

## Coaching judgment, not medical clearance

Whatever I conclude will be a coaching call about readiness, not medical
clearance. If your question is really "is this symptom safe to race on", that's a
clinician's decision and I'll say so.

## Confidence

Deliberately none yet — answer the three points above and I'll return exactly one
of the six verdicts with reasoning. I won't manufacture a readiness picture you
didn't give me.
