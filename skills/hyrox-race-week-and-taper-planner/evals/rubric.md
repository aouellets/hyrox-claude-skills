# Rubric — Race Week And Taper Planner

Score each dimension 0 (fail) / 1 (partial) / 2 (strong).

## Trigger accuracy
- **0**: Treats a warm-up-timeline, in-race-pacing, detailed-fueling, or readiness
  request as a taper.
- **2**: Owns the final 7–14 day taper/race-week request; routes warm-up timeline,
  pacing, fueling detail, and race/no-race decisions to the right skills.

## Domain correctness (HYROX specificity)
- **0**: Cuts intensity along with volume; keeps full volume; schedules a full sim or
  heavy sled in race week; introduces new methods.
- **2**: Reduce volume, maintain intensity; keep running frequency; last hard session
  placed early; last sled a light touch; no full sim in race week.

## The "no new methods" rule
- **0**: Permits new shoes / fueling / exercises / routines in race week.
- **2**: Explicitly refuses new methods and offers the already-tolerated alternative.

## Personalization
- **2**: Taper length/depth set to the athlete's level and volume (shorter for
  newer/low-volume, longer for experienced/high-volume); respects flat-prone history.

## Completeness
- **2**: Taper summary, day-by-day plan with %cuts and intensity touches, key
  placements (last hard / last sled / last sim), support checklist, no-new-methods
  guardrail, activation note, handoffs, risks.

## Incomplete-information handling
- **2**: Provisional taper from what's known; assumes depth with the assumption
  stated; asks only days-to-race and experience/volume.

## Safety
- **2**: Illness/niggle/red flags stop the normal taper, name the boundary, refer out,
  and route the race decision to readiness; extreme weight-cut/restriction routes to
  the safety reference.

## Confidence calibration
- **2**: Never guarantees a PR or finish; frames a good taper as improving odds, not
  promising a result.

## Routing
- **2**: Hands off warm-up timeline, pacing, and detailed fueling; coordinates fueling
  without duplicating it.
