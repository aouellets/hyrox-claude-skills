# Example 03 — Adversarial: new shoes, a new fueling product, and a hard "confidence" session in race week

## Request

> Race is in 5 days. I just bought a pair of carbon racing shoes I want to debut, and
> a new gel brand everyone's hyping — I'll do my first long carb-load with them. Also
> I'm nervous so I want to smash one really hard full simulation tomorrow to prove I'm
> ready. Build my race week around that.

## Response (excerpt)

I'd stop you on every one of these — not to be difficult, but because each breaks the
one rule that matters most this close to a race: **no new methods in race week.** New
gear and a hard sim 4 days out are how good blocks get sabotaged in the final stretch.

**1) New carbon shoes — not on race day.** Unfamiliar shoes change your foot strike and
load distribution; debuting them over 8 km of running plus stations risks blisters,
calf/Achilles soreness, or worse. **Race in the shoes you've already broken in.** If
you love the new pair, that's a post-race project.

**2) New gel brand + first-ever carb-load this week — no.** Race week is the worst time
to test a new fueling product: GI distress mid-race is a real and common consequence of
"trying it on the day." Use the fueling you've already trained with. *Detailed*
race-week fueling and carb timing is its own skill
(`hyrox-fueling-and-hydration-planner`) — but the rule there is the same: nothing new.
(If "first long carb-load" is part of an extreme weight or restriction pattern, that's a
health conversation, not a performance one —
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).)

**3) A hard full simulation 4 days out — no.** A full sim is too costly to recover from
and does **not** belong in race week; it would leave your legs flat on race day, the
opposite of what a taper is for. The nerves are normal — the fix is a **short, sharp**
race-pace touch (e.g. 4 × 400 m at race pace with full recovery) that builds confidence
without the fatigue. Any full sim should have been *before* the taper.

**What your last 5 days should actually look like (provisional, depth pending your
experience level):**

- Reduce volume to ~40–50%, keep short race-effort touches.
- One light shakeout + strides most days; rest as needed.
- Sleep prioritized; logistics (known shoes, known fuel, kit, route) done by −2.
- Rehearse your **existing** day-of warm-up — timeline owned by
  `hyrox-race-day-warmup-builder`.

**Routing summary:** fueling detail → `hyrox-fueling-and-hydration-planner` (same "no
new product" rule); warm-up timeline → `hyrox-race-day-warmup-builder`; pacing →
`hyrox-race-pacing-planner`. No promises on a finish time, and nothing new this week.
