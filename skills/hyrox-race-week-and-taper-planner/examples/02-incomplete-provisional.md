# Example 02 — Incomplete data: provisional taper with one targeted ask

## Request

> My HYROX race is next week. How should I taper?

## Response (excerpt)

I can give you a **provisional** taper now and sharpen it with one answer. I won't
invent your training history — I'll flag what I'm assuming.

**What materially changes the taper (please confirm):**

- **Your experience / typical weekly volume.** This sets how *deep* the taper goes:
  newer or lower-volume athletes need a **shorter, gentler** taper (too much rest
  leaves them flat); experienced, high-volume athletes benefit from a **deeper** one.

I don't need exact mileage — just roughly where you sit.

**Provisional 7-day taper (assumed: ~7 days out, recreational/moderate volume,
no niggle — all flagged):**

- Mechanism: **reduce volume, keep intensity.** Cut session length/load to ~50% by
  race day; keep running frequency roughly normal with a few short race-pace touches.
- **Days −7/−6:** the last slightly-harder touch (e.g. 3 × 5 min at race effort), then
  everything gets shorter and easier.
- **Day −5 or −6:** one **last light, sharp sled touch**, then leave the sled alone.
- **Days −4 to −1:** easy short runs + strides, a couple of race-pace pickups, rest as
  needed; **no full simulation** this week.
- **Sleep** prioritized all week; **mobility** kept normal (nothing new); **logistics**
  sorted early.

**The non-negotiable rule:** **no new methods in race week** — no new shoes, fueling,
exercises, or routines. Whatever you do this week should be things your body already
knows.

**Assumptions flagged:** days-out (~7), experience (moderate), and absence of a niggle
are all assumed. Tell me your volume/experience and I'll set the depth. Detailed race
fueling is a separate skill (`hyrox-fueling-and-hydration-planner`); the day-of warm-up
timeline is `hyrox-race-day-warmup-builder`.

**Scope note:** coaching, not medical advice; illness or pain before race day routes to
[safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md) and
`hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`.
