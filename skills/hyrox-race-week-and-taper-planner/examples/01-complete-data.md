# Example 01 — Complete data: 10-day taper for an experienced athlete

## Request

> Race is in 10 days. I'm an experienced singles athlete, ~55 km/week running plus 3
> strength/station sessions, last race 1:08. My last hard brick was 4 days ago and I
> did a full simulation 12 days ago. I tend to feel flat if I rest too much. Plan my
> taper.

## Response (excerpt)

**Taper summary.** Experienced, high-volume athlete → a **longer, more pronounced
~10-day taper**, but because you go flat with too much rest, we keep intensity touches
and running *frequency* high and cut **length/load**, not days. Target volume by race
week ≈ **−50% vs. peak**, intensity preserved. Mechanism: reduce volume, maintain
intensity; **no new methods** all week.

**Key placements.** Your last full sim (12 days out) is correctly *before* the taper —
good, no full sim this week. Last hard brick was 4 days ago; the **last genuinely hard
session** is the one below at the *start* of this window (day −10/−9), nothing hard in
the final 3–4 days. **Last sled exposure:** one light, sharp touch on day −8, then
leave it alone.

**Day-by-day (final 10 days, % vs. normal session):**

| Day | Session | Volume |
|-----|---------|--------|
| −10 | Threshold: 3 × 6 min @ race effort + strides | ~70% |
| −9  | Easy run 35 min + 4 strides | ~60% |
| −8  | Short sharp brick: 3 × (600 m @ race pace → light sled 12 m) — **last sled** | ~60% |
| −7  | Easy 30 min or rest | ~50% |
| −6  | Easy run 30 min + 4 strides | ~50% |
| −5  | Mini sharpener: 4 × 400 m @ race pace, full recovery | ~45% |
| −4  | Easy 25 min, very relaxed | ~40% |
| −3  | Rest or 20 min shakeout + 3 strides | ~35% |
| −2  | Easy 20 min + 3 short race-pace pickups; rehearse the day-of warm-up | ~30% |
| −1  | Rest / 10–15 min legs-open shakeout; logistics done | minimal |

**Support checklist.**
- **Sleep:** the priority lever — protect it from −10, not just the night before
  (nerves often disrupt −1).
- **Mobility:** keep your normal routine; **do not** add a new aggressive stretch/
  release this week.
- **Hydration/fueling:** normal habits; basics only — detailed race fueling/carb timing
  is `hyrox-fueling-and-hydration-planner`.
- **Logistics:** shoes already broken in, kit/chalk sorted by −3, venue route and start
  time confirmed.

**No new methods guardrail.** This week: no new shoes, no first-time fueling product,
no new mobility tool, no "extra hard session to feel sharp." All add risk, no upside.

**Activation note.** Rehearse your day-of warm-up on −2 so it's familiar; the
minute-by-minute timeline itself is `hyrox-race-day-warmup-builder`.

**Handoffs.** Pacing on the day → `hyrox-race-pacing-planner`; warm-up timeline →
`hyrox-race-day-warmup-builder`; fueling detail → `hyrox-fueling-and-hydration-planner`.

**Risks & referral.** No niggle reported → one-line scope note. Any sharp/worsening
pain or illness before race day is a readiness/medical question, not a taper one
([safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md);
`hyrox-race-readiness-decision-tool`).
