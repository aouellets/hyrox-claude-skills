# Example 01 — Complete: athlete supplies the current rule text for their season

**Request:**

> "My event is the 2026 season. For Mixed Doubles, my partner and I are arguing
> about the wall ball station. Here is the exact text from the rule sheet our event
> organizer sent us: *'In Doubles and Mixed Doubles, partners may freely divide the
> wall ball repetitions in any split, switching as often as they like, provided
> only one partner works at a time.'* So — can we switch mid-set as much as we want?"

---

### Question restated

Mixed Doubles, 2026 season: may partners switch freely during the wall ball
station, in any rep split, as long as only one works at a time?

### Season / source in use

- **Season:** 2026 (as stated by the athlete).
- **Source:** athlete-supplied rule text from their event organizer. Recording it
  to the registry it would carry, for example:
  - `source_id`: `user-2026-mixed-doubles-wallball`
  - `season`: `2026`
  - `effective_date`: *the date on the athlete's rule sheet — please confirm*
  - `retrieved_date`: today
  - `authorization_status`: `user_supplied`
  - `official_source_reference`: the organizer's rule sheet you received (I have
    not retrieved it myself).

### Labeled answer

**OFFICIAL RULE** (per your supplied 2026 text): Yes. The text you pasted states
partners *may freely divide the wall ball repetitions in any split, switching as
often as they like, provided only one partner works at a time.* So within this
station you can switch mid-set as much as you want, with the one constraint that
**only one of you is working at any moment** — no simultaneous reps.

**INTERPRETATION** (mine, flagged as such): "only one partner works at a time"
means the resting partner must be fully off the ball before the other begins; a
sloppy overlap during a swap could be judged as two working at once. Keep swaps
clean and unambiguous. This reading is *my interpretation* of your text, not extra
rulebook wording.

### Staleness warning

Low concern here: the text is from your own event's organizer and you have stated
it is the 2026 season, which matches your event. The only caveat is to confirm the
**effective_date** on the sheet so we know it is the version in force on race day —
organizers occasionally issue updates.

### What to verify

Confirm the date/version stamp on the rule sheet, and that no later 2026
amendment supersedes it. If your event publishes a more recent rules document,
that one governs.
