# Example 03 — Routing & safety: over-testing request + eccentric-load caution

## Request

> I'm brand new to all this. Give me a 20-test max-effort battery I can smash in
> two days — max sled, max lunges to failure, max-out everything — so I know
> exactly where I stand before I start.

## Response (abridged)

I'm going to push back on the design, because as written it's both more testing
than you need and a recovery/safety risk for a brand-new athlete.

**Why I'm not building the 20-test smash:**
- **Minimum useful beats exhaustive.** A handful of standardized tests tells your
  coach almost everything 20 do, and you can actually recover and train after them.
- **Maximal eccentric volume in unaccustomed athletes is a rhabdomyolysis risk.**
  Max sled and lunges-to-failure stacked in two days is exactly the unaccustomed
  heavy-eccentric pattern that can cause it. Warning signs to take seriously:
  severe muscle pain/swelling out of proportion to the session, profound weakness,
  and dark/cola-colored urine — if any appear, seek prompt medical evaluation.
  This is a coaching caution, not a diagnosis.

**What I'd run instead (minimum useful, submaximal-biased):**
- 5 km run TT, SkiErg 1000 m, Row 1000 m, sled push/pull at *moderate* load
  (technique + a comparable baseline, not a max), 100 wall balls (or proxy),
  farmers hold, and one compromised-run test.
- Spread across 2–3 short sessions, sleds and lunges last, full warm-up logged.
- Lunges: a *quality* set to a controlled stop, not to failure, for a novice.

**Routing.** Interpretation of the results → `hyrox-athlete-assessment`. If you
want full race rehearsals later (not now, as a beginner) →
`hyrox-simulation-builder`. If anything hurts during testing, stop the loaded
test and, for a red-flag symptom, see an appropriate professional rather than
pushing through.
