# Example 02 — Incomplete data: limited equipment, time unstated

## Request

> What benchmarks should I do? I only have a treadmill, a rower, and some
> dumbbells at home — no sled, no ski-erg, no wall ball.

## Response (abridged)

This is a **provisional** battery, because I'm missing your weekly time, division,
and race date, and your equipment can't cover every station directly. I'll state
the assumptions rather than pretend the gaps aren't there.

**Stated assumptions (correct me if wrong):** ~3–4 h/week; baseline purpose;
no sled/ski-erg/wall-ball access at home.

**What you CAN test directly:**

| Test | Measures | Score |
|---|---|---|
| 5 km treadmill TT (1% grade) | run threshold | time + pace/km |
| Row 1000 m | erg power/pacing | time |
| Compromised-run test (DB thruster → treadmill) | running off legs | per-run pace + cadence recovery |
| 100 DB goblet thrusters for time | wall-ball *substitute* endurance marker | time + breaks |

**What you CANNOT properly baseline at home (flagged, not faked):**
- **SkiErg** — no direct test; I won't substitute a rower number for it as if it
  were equivalent.
- **Sled push/pull** — no sled; a DB carry is not a sled and I won't pretend it
  predicts your sled time. The friction and load are too different.
- **Wall balls** — DB thrusters are a *rough* endurance proxy only, not a wall-ball
  baseline; label it as a proxy when you record it.

**Next step / routing.** To baseline the missing stations, you need one session
somewhere with the equipment (a HYROX-equipped gym or a sled). When you can,
re-run this with sled load and ski-erg access and I'll complete the battery. Tell
me your weekly time and race date and I'll set the retest cadence — those two
materially change the schedule; nothing else here does. Interpretation of these
results goes to `hyrox-athlete-assessment`.
