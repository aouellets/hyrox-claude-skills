# Example 1 — Complete data: an 8-week wall-ball capacity build

**User:**
> I keep failing on wall balls at the end of HYROX. Last race my legs were dead
> and I broke into tiny sets and lost ages. I can do 40 unbroken wall balls fresh
> to the standard target. I train 4 days/week, have a wall-ball target and a
> rack at home, race in 8 weeks. Squat 1RM ~140 kg. Build me a wall-ball program.

**Response:**

## Station & demand read
Wall balls — repeated loaded squat-to-throw; quads, shoulders, full-body
coordination, high rep count, performed last on the most fatigued legs of the day.
Your stated limiter is **not fresh capacity** (40 unbroken is solid) but
**repeatability under whole-race leg fatigue and a break strategy that spiraled**.
That is a conditioning + pacing problem, so the program targets fatigued-leg
volume and a planned set/break structure, not raw strength. Confidence: medium-high
(one race observation + a fresh number).

## Current capacity & assumptions
- Fresh unbroken: 40 reps to standard target. Race performance: broke into "tiny
  sets" with large losses — exact rep count unknown, labeled an assumption.
- 8 weeks out, 4 days/week, target + rack available, squat ~140 kg (ample leg
  strength relative to the throw — confirms this is conditioning, not strength).
- Total race rep count is **division/season-dependent**; I have not assumed a
  number. The plan is built in relative terms (% of your fresh unbroken set and
  planned break structure) and applies to whatever the season's count is.

## Prerequisites
- A competent front-loaded squat to the depth standard and a stable overhead catch
  → your squat number says these are present. Maintain ankle/hip mobility for depth.

## Technique standards (coachable, not a fault diagnosis)
- Consistent depth to standard each rep; full target contact; a rhythmic
  catch-into-squat so the catch loads the next descent. If you suspect a *specific*
  fault (e.g. you sense you're cutting depth as you fatigue), run
  `hyrox-technique-and-fault-analyzer` and bring the confirmed fault back here.

## Strength & efficiency targets
- No added barbell strength needed; maintenance only. Efficiency target: hold a
  planned set size at RPE 7–8 with depth/target intact, no failure.

## Progression ladder (advance on criterion, regression on hand)
| Wk | Key fatigued-leg session | Progression criterion | Regression |
|----|--------------------------|-----------------------|------------|
| 1–2 | 5×15 wall balls, 45 s rest, after a 3-min easy run | All sets to standard, RPE ≤7 | 5×12 |
| 3–4 | 4×20 after a sled-push or lunge effort (leg pre-fatigue) | Depth holds on fatigued legs | drop pre-fatigue, 4×15 |
| 5–6 | 3×25–30 after a 600 m compromised run | No failure, ≤2 s/rep slowdown | 3×20 |
| 7 | Race-rep total in planned sets (e.g. 30/20/15/… or your chosen splits) off cooked legs | Completes within target time, no spiral | smaller planned sets |
| 8 | Taper: 1× half-volume rehearsal of the break plan only | Crisp execution | — |

## Accessory & conditioning
- **Break-plan rehearsal** is the core: pre-decide sets (e.g. front-load larger,
  then steady) so you never default to "tiny sets." 2×/week.
- Accessory: split squats 3×8/leg RPE 7, and a shoulder-endurance set
  (light DB push press 3×15) for catch durability.
- Repeatability conditioning: the run-then-wall-ball bricks above train the real
  demand — wall balls on legs already cooked by running and prior stations.

## Proficiency test, race benchmark & retest
- **Test now (Wk 1):** max unbroken on fresh legs, then again after a 600 m run.
  The drop quantifies your compromised-leg gap.
- **Race benchmark (Wk 7):** complete your planned race-rep total off a 600 m
  compromised run in your target station time with the planned break structure.
- **Retest date:** end of Wk 6, repeat the Wk-1 compromised test; target a smaller
  fresh-vs-compromised drop.

## Integration & adjustment
Slot the fatigued-leg wall-ball work on a quality day, not the day after your
hardest run. If legs feel under-recovered, take the regression — it is the plan,
not a failure. For the *whole-race* break strategy and pacing into the final
station, also see `hyrox-wall-ball-completion-planner` and
`hyrox-race-pacing-planner`.

*Scope note: ordinary end-of-race leg fatigue is the trained target; sharp or
persistent knee pain is not — see the safety framework.*
