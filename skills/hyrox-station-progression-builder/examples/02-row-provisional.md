# Example 2 — Incomplete data: provisional row build, baseline test first

**User:**
> Can you build me a rowing program for HYROX? My row is my weak station.

**Response:**

This is a **provisional** rowing build — I'm missing the two numbers that would
make it specific, so it opens with a baseline test and stays in relative terms
until you fill them in.

## What I'm missing (and why it matters)
1. **Your current row capacity** — a recent ~1000 m time, or your 500 m split and
   how it holds up when fatigued. This sets the starting intensity and the
   progression criteria. Without it I will not invent a split.
2. **Weeks to race (or general build)** — sets how aggressively to progress.

## Station & demand read
Row (~1000 m) — aerobic/glycolytic, **leg-drive-led** full-body pull; trunk and
grip. The usual limiters are over-pulling with the arms, rushing the recovery,
pacing too hard, and a drag setting that doesn't suit you
([station-taxonomy](../../../references/station-taxonomy.md)). "Weak station" most
often means a pacing/efficiency limiter rather than raw power — but I'd confirm
with the baseline test before assuming.

## Provisional program (relative until you give me a baseline)
**Week 1 — baseline (do this first):**
- 1000 m row for time at a hard-but-even effort; log the 500 m split and stroke
  rate. Then 4×250 m at the same target split, 1 min rest — note where the split
  slips. This is your starting point; the rest of the plan keys off it.

**Provisional progression (replace "X" with your tested 1000 m split):**
| Block | Session | Criterion to advance |
|-------|---------|----------------------|
| Aerobic | 3×1000 m at X+4–6 s/500 m, easy strokes ~24–26 spm | Even splits, RPE ≤7 |
| Threshold | 5–6×500 m at X, 1 min rest | Hold X across all reps |
| Repeatability | 4×(500 m row + 200 m run) at race effort | Row split holds with run attached |

- **Technique standards:** legs-then-back-then-arms sequencing, controlled
  recovery slower than the drive, an even rate. (If you suspect a *specific* fault
  like opening the back early, run `hyrox-technique-and-fault-analyzer`.)
- **Drag/damper:** experiment in the baseline; most HYROX athletes settle into a
  moderate drag rather than maxing it — confirm by feel, don't copy a number.

## Retest
Repeat the Week-1 baseline after ~4 weeks; target a faster, more even 1000 m and a
smaller fade in the 4×250 m.

**Send me your current ~1000 m row (or 500 m split) and weeks to race and I'll turn
this into a dated, specific block.** Everything above stays valid; only the
intensities sharpen.
