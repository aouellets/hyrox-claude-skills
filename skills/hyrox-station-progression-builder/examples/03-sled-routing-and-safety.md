# Example 3 — Routing + safety: sled deferral and an eccentric-load red flag

**User:**
> Build me a sled-push conditioning block. Also — after my last heavy sandbag-lunge
> session two days ago my quads are still really swollen and painful, way worse
> than normal DOMS, and my pee was dark this morning.

**Response:**

**Read the second part first — this takes priority over any programming.**

### Safety: this needs medical evaluation before more training
Severe muscle pain and swelling out of proportion to the session, after heavy
unaccustomed eccentric work (sandbag lunges are a classic trigger), **plus
dark/cola-colored urine**, are the warning signs of **rhabdomyolysis**. That is
outside coaching scope. Please stop training that area and seek prompt evaluation
by a medical professional today — this is not something to program around or
"push through." I won't prescribe a sled or lunge block while these symptoms are
present. (See [safety-and-referral](../../../references/safety-and-referral.md).)

When you've been cleared, come back and we'll rebuild load gradually.

### On the sled-push block — routing, not a brush-off
Even setting the symptoms aside, **sled push has its own specialist skill** in this
pack and I'd route you there rather than give you a shallower version here:

→ **`hyrox-sled-performance-specialist`** covers sled push and pull in depth:
strength base, starting mechanics, posture and body angle, hand position,
footwork, acceleration vs sustained drive, turns, and — importantly — the fact
that **gym sled load is not race resistance** (turf, flooring, sled model,
friction, calibration, and distance all change the effective resistance). I would
only *sequence* sled work into a broader multi-station block from here.

So once you're medically cleared:
- For the **sled push itself** → `hyrox-sled-performance-specialist`.
- For **fitting sled + other stations into your weeks** → come back here, or
  `hyrox-training-plan-builder` for the whole plan.
- If a fault is showing up (e.g. you stall mid-push) → `hyrox-technique-and-fault-analyzer`.

I've deliberately not written the block yet — the symptoms come first.
