# Example 2 — Incomplete data: no-sled request, equipment list missing

**User:**
> My gym got rid of the sled. How do I keep training the sled push?

**Response:**

This is a **provisional** answer — I can give you the *approach* now, but the actual
swap depends on one thing you haven't told me yet.

## What I need before I commit to a swap
**What equipment do you actually have?** (Home, commercial gym, travel? Leg press?
A heavy prowler-alternative? Bands/straps? A loadable carry?) The feasible substitute
for a sled push depends entirely on this — I won't pick one blind.

## And the honest caveat up front
Whatever we land on, it will be a **substitute, not an equivalent**, and the sled is
the **worst case** for that: **turf, flooring, sled model, friction/wear, calibration,
and distance change the effective resistance**, so **no implement at any load is "the
same as" the race sled.** I will not equate a gym swap with race resistance, and I
won't assert a race sled weight from memory (that's season/division-dependent —
`hyrox-division-and-rules-advisor`).

## Station demand spec (sled push) — what the swap must chase
- **Joint action:** continuous low-angle leg drive.
- **Force orientation:** **horizontal** (the hard axis to reproduce without a sled).
- **Contraction type:** concentric leg drive, high systemic cost.
- **Energy-system load:** alactic/glycolytic max-effort leg drive.
- **Grip demand:** low. **Compromised-run transfer:** one of the worst leg-flooders.
- **Most important to preserve:** **horizontal leg drive + energy-system load.**

## Likely candidates (pending your kit)
- **Heavy leg press / heavy walking lunges** — preserve the leg-drive and energy cost;
  **fail** the horizontal force orientation.
- **Band-resisted or partner-resisted marches/sprints** — preserve horizontal drive
  better; **fail** the magnitude of resistance.
- **A loaded carry pushed hard uphill / treadmill incline push** — partial horizontal
  transfer; **fails** true sled friction.

I'll pick and dose the best one once you tell me what's available.

## Close-out
This swap **holds** the leg-drive quality; it does not reproduce sled friction. When
you have a real sled again, go to `hyrox-sled-performance-specialist` for the actual
push mechanics and repeatability — that's where the real coaching lives.

**Tell me your available equipment and I'll lock the swap, the PRESERVES/FAILS, and the
sets/reps/RPE.**
