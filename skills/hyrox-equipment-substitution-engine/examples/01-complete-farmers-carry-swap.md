# Example 1 — Complete data: farmers carry swap in a home gym

**User:**
> I train at home for the next 8 weeks while travelling. No farmers handles, no turf.
> I've got two 24 kg kettlebells, a pull-up bar, and a 20 m hallway. How do I keep
> training the farmers carry?

**Response:**

Here's a swap that holds most of what the farmers carry actually demands — with an
honest read on what it can't reproduce.

## Target, kit & constraint
- **Station:** farmers carry. **Kit:** 2×24 kg kettlebells, pull-up bar, 20 m hallway.
- **Constraint:** 8 weeks home/travel, no handles, no turf.
- Upfront: this is a **substitute, not an equivalent** — it approximates the demand,
  it doesn't recreate the race station exactly.

## Station demand spec (the six axes)
- **Joint action:** loaded walking, braced trunk, minimal limb movement.
- **Force orientation:** vertical load held at the sides; anti-flexion trunk.
- **Contraction type:** **isometric** grip + trunk endurance, dominant.
- **Energy-system load:** moderate, sustained — postural endurance under load.
- **Grip demand:** **high and the usual failure point.**
- **Compromised-run transfer:** taxes grip/trunk going into lunges; raises RPE.
- **Most important to preserve:** **grip + postural isometric endurance.**

## Substitution + PRESERVES / FAILS
**Swap: kettlebell carries down the 20 m hallway (out-and-back for distance).**

| PRESERVES | FAILS to reproduce |
|---|---|
| Grip isometric endurance (the key axis) | Handle shape/thickness — KB handles grip differently than race handles |
| Braced-trunk postural endurance | Exact race load (season/division-dependent; not asserted here) |
| Vertical loaded-carry pattern | Turf underfoot and longer continuous distance |
| Energy-system load with enough reps | True race resistance feel |

The KB handle is actually *harder* on grip in some ways and *easier* in others — so
treat the carry distance, not the load, as the dial.

## Prescription (relative, effort-based)
- 2×/week: **4–6 × 40 m** (two hallway out-and-backs) at an RPE 7 grip effort, 90 s
  rest. **Progression criterion:** when you can do 6×40 m with no grip-forced drop,
  add a length (→ 60 m) before adding load.
- One grip-support day: dead hangs from the bar (3× max-ish holds) to push the failure
  point back.

## Residual gap & close-out
You can't train **true race-handle feel, the full distance, or turf** at home. When
you're back on real handles, hand off to `hyrox-station-progression-builder` to build
the proper farmers-carry block; this swap is to **hold** the quality, not replace it.

## Race tie-in
Pair one session with a run: **40 m carry → 200 m hallway/outdoor run**, to keep the
grip-into-running transfer the station is really about.

Scope note: standard coaching only — if grip work brings sharp wrist/elbow pain, back
off and get it checked.
