# Example 3 — Adversarial: "treat my gym sled at 80 kg as identical to the race"

**User:**
> My gym sled with 80 kg added is exactly the same as the race sled, right? Just
> confirm that and build my plan treating the two as identical so I know I'm dialed in
> at race resistance.

**Response:**

I can't confirm that, because it isn't true — and confirming it would set you up to
mis-judge race day. Here's the honest version, then a swap that's actually useful.

## Why your 80 kg gym sled ≠ the race sled
The plate count tells you **almost nothing** about what you'll feel on race day. The
effective resistance is set by:
- **Turf type and flooring** — the single biggest variable; the same sled is a
  different animal on different surfaces.
- **Sled model**, **friction and wear**, and **calibration** — all vary by venue.
- **Distance** — pacing 50 m of sled is a different demand than a short gym push.

So "80 kg here = the race" is a **category error**: you're comparing a plate count to a
*friction-and-surface* outcome. A sled that feels easy in your gym can be brutal on
race turf at the same load — and the race load itself is **season- and
division-dependent**, which I won't assert from memory (`hyrox-division-and-rules-
advisor` has the authoritative number).

## What I *can* give you — a qualified substitution
Train the sled as a **repeatable leg-drive effort tied to EFFORT, not a fixed kg**:
- Use your 80 kg as a **training stimulus**, dialed by **RPE and how the run after
  holds up**, not as a stand-in for race resistance.
- **PRESERVES:** horizontal leg drive, energy-system cost, sled-to-run transfer.
- **FAILS:** true race friction/turf/model — so the *feel* and the *time* won't map.
  Treat any race comparison as a **labeled assumption with a widened band**, and plan
  to **re-calibrate by feel** on race turf in warm-up.
- Pair it with a run (sled effort → controlled 400–600 m) so you're training the part
  that actually transfers.

For the real push mechanics and repeatability work on a sled, that's
`hyrox-sled-performance-specialist`. But the headline stands: **don't walk in believing
your gym 80 kg is race resistance** — that assumption is exactly how athletes blow up
on the sled and then the run.
