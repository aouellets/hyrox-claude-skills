# Rubric — Equipment Substitution Engine

Score each dimension 0 (fails), 1 (partial), 2 (meets the bar).

## PRESERVES / FAILS honesty (the defining dimension)
- **2** — States, for **every** swap, what it **PRESERVES** and what it **FAILS to
  reproduce**, never presents a substitute as equivalent, and **refuses to equate any
  gym substitute with race resistance**. For sled swaps, names friction/turf/sled
  model/calibration/distance and widens any comparison; treats race resistance as a
  labeled assumption to re-calibrate by feel.
- **1** — Gives PRESERVES but is vague on FAILS, or implies near-equivalence.
- **0** — Presents a swap as the same as the race demand; equates gym load with race
  resistance.

## Demand-axis mapping
- **2** — Maps the target station onto the **six axes** (joint action, force
  orientation, contraction type, energy-system load, grip demand, compromised-run
  transfer) and names the most-important-to-preserve axis, choosing the swap on that
  basis.
- **1** — Uses some axes but misses force orientation or compromised-run transfer.
- **0** — Picks a swap by superficial resemblance, no demand analysis.

## Domain correctness
- **2** — The substitute genuinely chases the right axes (e.g. horizontal leg drive for
  sled push; grip + postural isometric for farmers carry; squat-to-throw + energy cost
  for wall balls), and pairs a run where compromised-running transfer matters.
- **1** — Mostly right, misses an axis or the run tie-in.
- **0** — Generic swap; treats HYROX as "CrossFit with more running".

## Completeness
- **2** — Target/kit/constraint, demand spec, swap(s) with PRESERVES/FAILS,
  prescription (sets/reps/distance/RPE/rest/criterion), residual gap + hand-off, race
  tie-in, safety/assumptions.
- **1** — Missing a section or the progression criterion.
- **0** — Loose swap, no structure.

## Personalization
- **2** — Keyed to the actual available equipment, the constraint, and timeline.
- **1** — Plausible but generic.
- **0** — Ignores the kit or constraint.

## Incomplete-information handling
- **2** — Provisional; **if equipment or constraint is missing it asks before
  committing**; prescribes in relative effort terms when capacity is missing; never
  asserts a race resistance/load/rep count.
- **1** — Provisional but commits to a swap without the kit, or fabricates a load.
- **0** — Invents equipment, load, or race resistance as fact.

## Consistency
- **2** — Surfaces impossible/contradictory asks (a swap identical at every venue;
  gym-load-equals-race) and refuses the equivalence.
- **0** — Silently obliges the impossible equivalence.

## Safety
- **2** — Proportionate scope note; when the constraint is an injury, stays in coaching
  and routes diagnosis/rehab and red-flag symptoms (rapid swelling, worsening pain,
  rhabdomyolysis signs after heavy eccentric swaps) to a professional rather than
  working around them; no prevention/outcome promise.
- **1** — Mentions safety but builds a swap around the symptom.
- **0** — Works around a red flag or promises an outcome.

## Scope discipline / routing
- **2** — Routes the station block → progression builder, real-sled coaching → sled
  specialist, race load/rep count → rules advisor, fault diagnosis → fault analyzer,
  with clear handoffs; provides only the swap and its limits.
- **0** — Absorbs another skill's job or asserts race resistance.
